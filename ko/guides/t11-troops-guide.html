라스트 워: 서바이벌 runs a strict three-way rock-paper-scissors combat system: 항공기 beats 탱크, 탱크 beats 미사일, 미사일 beats 항공기, with each counter granting +20% damage dealt and −20% damage taken. 병력 come in the same three "vehicle" classes (탱크 / 항공기 / 미사일 차량 — no infantry/lancer/marksman split, that's Whiteout Survival), organized in tiers T1–T11 gated by 병영 level (T10 at 병영 30, T11 at 병영 35 plus 무기 연구소 research). Each troop has four battle stats — Attack, Defense, Health, Lethality — and is deployed as part of a 5-hero squad (2 front / 3 back) that gains stacking mono-type formation bonuses up to +20% Attack/Defense/HP for a full-type stack. Combat losses are triaged into three buckets: lightly wounded (auto-heal), wounded (병원), fainted / severely wounded (응급 센터 at fainted-unit ratio 21%→40%); anything past both caps is permanently killed. Rallies combine up to 5 얼라이언스 joiners under the leader's stats — the leader's research, gear, hero levels, and drone bonuses apply to every joiner's troops, so leader selection is the single largest lever in 얼라이언스 combat (see 08-얼라이언스-systems for 랠리-coordination context).

T11 — 무기 연구소 and specializations

T11 troops (branded "Level 11 Unit" in-game) shipped in the Age of 오일 season. To unlock (cpt-hedge t11 guide; ldshop t11 guide; earnne support guide; official firstfun zendesk):

  1. 시즌 4 must be completed (off-season phase)
  2. HQ ≥ 27 to unlock the 무기 연구소 (single-level, non-upgradable building)
  3. 병영 ≥ 35 to actually train T11
  4. All four 무기 연구소 researches to 100%, completed in strict order:
  5. Helmet — required first
  6. Body 장갑 (장갑)
  7. Protective Gear (Boots in some translations)
  8. 무기

Every level takes Armament Materials; every 5th level of the first three branches (and every 1군–3rd level of 무기) additionally requires an Armament Core. Final unlock consumes 120,000 Armament Materials + 200 Armament Cores + 1.2M 오일.

Timeline expectations (earnne):

  • 무과금: 4–5 months
  • Light spenders: 2–3 months
  • Heavy spenders: 1–2 months

T11 has two specializations you can swap between every 72 hours:

Specialization패시브Best for
장갑 보병Battle Protection — severe wound rate reduced by 5%Defense, war zones, situations where hospital + emergency center overflow
돌격 병철 Will — penalty for not being at full march capacity reduced by 10%Offense, rallies you rejoin without refilling, running less-than-full marches

Base stats are identical between the two — only the passive differs. Stat gains vs. T10: roughly 5–10% across Attack, Defense, HP, 편성 Load, 사기, and Power (earnne).

Armament star bonuses (require all four branches at the same star level to activate):

  • ★1: Both types ignore part of enemy "Reduce Damage Taken" effects
  • ★2: Counterattack / restoration effects
  • ★3: Increased Lethality rate when attacking
  • ★4: 장갑 보병 further reduces severe wound rate; 돌격 병 further reduces the not-full-capacity penalty

Note: 퍼스트펀's official developer guidance (firstfungroup zendesk) explicitly says that in World Map PvP where healing cycles fast, the 5% severe wound reduction is not a large practical advantage — implying 돌격 병 is the default for most players unless running a pure defense meta.

T11 stat delta over T10 — post-launch cross-check (2026-07-15). Both T11 specializations share identical base stats — the specialization only changes the passive proc (Battle Protection vs 철 Will), not the underlying Attack/Defense/HP/Lethality numbers. Post-launch stat delta over maxed T10 has two competing published estimates: (a) an early Lootbar preview article speculated +18–22% Attack, +12% HP and was explicitly flagged as pre-release rumor at the time; (b) post-launch guide-aggregator sources (per Last War Handbook troop-tier comparison, search excerpt 2026-07-15) now cite the effective post-launch delta at closer to +5–10% Attack/HP over T10. Reconciliation: the Lootbar preview was likely including Armament star bonuses (★1–★4) in the "T11 stats" number, while the post-launch aggregator numbers are base-T11-only. Neither source publishes a per-stat table (Attack / Defense / HP / Lethality) with exact integers — the definitive numbers still require an in-game pull. See Open Questions.
