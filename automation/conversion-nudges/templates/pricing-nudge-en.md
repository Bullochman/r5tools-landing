You looked at the pricing page yesterday and didn't upgrade. That's fine — most people don't buy on the first look. But I want to answer the question people always ask me on Discord before deciding:

**"Is $30 really worth it for one 8-week season?"**

Honest answer: it depends on your role.

**If you're a regular player** — no. Free tier covers you. Use it for coal timing and freeze checks and move on. I'll never nag you.

**If you're R4/R5 or a march commander** — the answer is almost always yes, and here's why: one bad landing choice, one missed freeze, or one under-buffed march in KE/Fortress costs your alliance more than $30 in resources every single time. The paid tier has the calculators that stop those specific mistakes. That's the whole pitch.

**Concrete example.** A WZ 2007 R5 last season used the paid Coal Burn stacker to plan a KE march. He was over-provisioning by 22% (typical). Saved ~4.8M coal on that one event. Coal was going for ~$1 per 100k in gems that week. You do the math.

If you're not R4/R5, [stay on free](https://r5tools.io/) — genuinely. The tools are still useful.

If you are, [$30 → paid personal](https://r5tools.io/#pricing) or [$50 → alliance bundle for everyone](https://r5tools.io/#alliance-bundle).
