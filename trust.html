Non-affiliation with the game studio

r5tools.io is NOT the game. We are not Last War Games, First Fun, Century Games, or any of their partners. We do not have insider access. We do not have advance patch notes. We do not represent the game publisher in any way.

All in-game names, unit stats, and screenshots are used to help you plan around the game. That is nominative fair use under U.S. copyright law. If a rights-holder disagrees, we will hear you out immediately — email above.

Security posture (what we DON'T touch)

We never ask for your in-game credentials. Not your username. Not your password. Not your session token. Nothing that could log into your account. If any r5tools page ever asks, close the tab and email us — it is a phishing attempt using our name.

No automation

We do not click, tap, or send anything to the game on your behalf. Our tools are calculators and planners, not bots.

No memory reads

We do not read your device's game memory, network traffic, or clipboard. Everything we know comes from data you paste in or upload.

No ToS violations

Nothing we do would be against LWS Terms of Service that we are aware of. We are, at most, a spreadsheet with a UI.

Photos stay local

Profile Studio processes 100% in your browser. Photos never touch our servers.

Encrypted in transit

TLS 1.2 or higher everywhere. Access codes stored as hashes, not plaintext.

You can delete anything

Email us with your alliance code and we wipe within 30 days — usually within 24 hours.

Bug bounty program

If you find a security vulnerability, please tell us before you tell the internet. We are a solo shop and we take security reports seriously.

How to report

  • Email evanjones@mdr.net with subject "Security".
  • Include: what you found, how to reproduce, and (if you want) your name / handle for the hall of fame.
  • Please do not publicly disclose until we confirm a fix.

What we consider in scope

  • Authentication or session bugs on r5tools.io and access-codes.r5tools.io.
  • Ways to read another alliance's uploaded data.
  • Cross-site scripting (XSS), SQL injection, SSRF, IDOR on our origins.
  • Bypasses of access-code entitlement checks.

Out of scope

  • Third-party services (report those directly to the vendor: Stripe, Cloudflare, GitHub Pages, Railway).
  • Rate limit or brute-force reports without a proof of concept.
  • Missing security headers with no exploit.
  • Vulnerabilities requiring physical access to a user's device.
