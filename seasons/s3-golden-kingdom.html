TL;DR

Last War: Survival runs on an 8-week seasonal treadmill: every server rotates through a fresh themed map with new resources, buildings, PvP events, and hero/weapon rollouts. Since Season 2 (Polar Storm) ended, First Fun has shipped four full seasons — S3 Golden Kingdom (Egyptian desert / Spice Wars / Curse Resistance), S4 Evernight Isle (Sakura island / Lighthouse / Blood Night), S5 Wild West (Golden Wasteland / Banks / Trade Train / merged 8-warzone map), and S6 Shadow Rainforest (faction warfare / permanent city destruction / Hero Awakening). Season 7 is confirmed by community creators as launching approximately August 2026 but no official theme/mechanic details have leaked publicly at the time of this research (2026-07-14). Each season introduces a new seasonal resource loop, a new PvP conflict pattern, and a designated hero for UR ascension or Awakening — and the trend from S5→S6 is toward fewer, longer war windows and permanent, high-stakes territorial outcomes rather than continuous grind (documented in 13-meta-competitive).

Core mechanics

The seasonal cadence — what's fixed across every season

Since Season 3, every LWS season has followed the same structural template, with only theme and specific mechanics varying:

  • 2-week pre-season (introduced in S4, retained through S6): countdown, faction/warzone grouping, rule preview, reward preview, Season Boost, capitol conquest without adjacency, Alliance Safe Time configuration.
  • 7-day Season Preview — daily challenges unlock the season's mechanics one at a time before main season begins.
  • 8-week main season — the actual war phase, divided into weekly milestones (city unlocks, faction contests, war windows).
  • Celebration / off-season week — final rewards, T-troop unlocks (T11 introduced in S4), migration prep to next season.
  • Server migration — at season end, players' accounts, HQ level, heroes, and tech carry forward; the map and all seasonal buildings/resources reset. Servers merge across warzones as populations decay (08-alliance-systems cross-ref).

The 8-week main season subdivides consistently: Weeks 1–3 are build-out (buildings unlocked progressively, seasonal resource stockpiling), Weeks 4–6 are the main PvP conflict (Spice Wars / Copper Wars / City Clash / Faction Duel prelims), and Weeks 7–8 are the season-defining endgame (Capitol War, Ancestral Temple, Faction Duel final).

Length has crept upward: S3 was documented as ~8 weeks; S4 as "roughly 8–10 weeks" including celebration; total cycle including pre-season, main, and post-season is ~14 weeks per KR namu-wiki interpretation. Sources disagree on whether S4/S5 celebration weeks count as "the season" or as inter-season buffer.

Season 3 — Golden Kingdom (Egyptian desert)

Launch: ~6–7 weeks after Season 2 concludes on a given server (rolling per-server launch, not a global date). Pre-season 14-day countdown appears below the VS duel button. Duration: 8 weeks. Theme: Noah has crowned himself ruler of the "Golden Kingdom" of ancient Egypt, unearthed the Monument of the Dead, and unleashed a mummy army and sandworms.

Map: 13×13 grid. Three building types populate the desert:

  • Cities (Levels 1–6, produce 100–600 Spice/hour)
  • Digging Strongholds (produce Mithril)
  • Trade Posts (individually captured; Levels 1–5 unlock progressively weeks 2–3)

Alliance Center secondary building unlocks:

  • Level 5: Mithril Plant (34,560/hour)
  • Level 10: Exploratory Well (600 Oil/hour)
  • Level 15: Sacred Water Hut (8,640/hour)
  • Level 20: Obelisk (6 Protector Horns/hour)

Desert Protectors — the signature S3 combat mechanic. Disposable elite units converted from battlefield casualties (10% of losses recovered via Recon Plane assist). Progressive squad buffs based on stationed count:

  • 500+ units: Power of Adaptation — +250 Curse Resistance, +25% ATK/DEF/HP
  • 750+ units: Death Shockwave — damages enemy units when squad defeated
  • 1000+ units: Blue Flame Spell — 1-hour debuff on defeated enemy base
  • 1250+ units: Protector Rally — 3× march speed bonus (Protector squads only)

Each hero carries 1/10 of stationed protector count. All participating Protectors vanish after combat — this is a burnable resource, not a persistent army.

Curse Resistance — new stat that gates PvE content. If your Curse Resistance is below a monster's requirement, your damage drops sharply (up to 99.9% reduction). Rally leader's resistance applies to all participants unless individual members have higher levels. This is why the Curse Research Lab is the single most important S3 building to keep upgraded.

Curse Resistance formula precision (open question status 2026-07-16): Multiple sources confirm the qualitative shape — "damage drops to as much as 99.9%" when CR is under monster requirement — but the exact CR-shortfall → damage-multiplier table remains gated behind Cpt Hedgehog's paywalled Season 3 pages and the Last War Vault Curse Resistance guide's supporter-only content [lastwarvault.com/guides/season-3/curse-resistance-guide/ WebFetch 2026-07-16 confirmed paywall]. What the KB does know from published summaries: (a) the penalty is a smooth-ish curve, not a hard cliff — small CR shortfalls yield small damage cuts, large shortfalls yield the 99.9% wipe; (b) the rally leader's CR overrides participant CR only if the leader's is higher; (c) sandworm content is exempt from CR entirely. Downstream Season-3 tools should model CR as a rally-leader input with a "damage effectiveness" output of 100% if CR ≥ requirement, else scaled down toward 0.1% — the exact interpolation curve should be labeled TBD, from-in-game until a screenshot pull captures the S3 monster-CR-requirement table.

Sandworm Crisis event (14 days): Combat between alliances generates "shock waves"; accumulated shock waves spawn:

  • Large Sandworms — knock bases outside Alliance Center range to distant map locations (forced teleport)
  • Giant Sandworms — map-wide hazards, trapped via the Trap Landmine profession skill

Spice Wars (Weeks 4–8): Two factions battle twice weekly across stages — Waiting → Declaration → Invitation → Preparation → War → End. Attackers can steal up to 30% of enemy Spice by destroying secondary buildings (5% each) and the Alliance Center (10%).

Warlord Missile in Season 3 — larger blast than Season 2 (added 2026-07-16). The Warlord Missile mechanic ported from S2 fires a 35×35 tile Contaminated Land blast in S3 (vs S2's 25×25). Same 3-min prep window, same public-warlord-base broadcast during prep, same 72-hour cooldown on skill after successful use or intercept-cancel. The wider blast means the S2 "park 5 tiles from the Furnace" rule of thumb undershoots in S3 — plan Season-3 defense with a 10-tile clearance ring around any missile-targetable building. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 3 Spice Wars & Faction Duel search-excerpt 2026-07-16; lastwargame.online Season 3 mechanics search-excerpt 2026-07-16 — both Tier B; direct fetches 403]

Faction Duel (Week 7): Days 1–6 = Invasion Right Contest; Day 7 = Capitol War (controlling Capitol yields +10M Spice; each Cannon controlled = +5M Spice).

Oasis Greenification event: Staged expansion of desertified land. Weeks 1–4 restrict greenification to city perimeters; weeks 5–7 allow free expansion once regions cross 80% thresholds, unlocking level progression toward capitol control.

Alliance capture limits: 2 city war declarations/day, max 8 captured cities; 2 digging stronghold captures/day, max 8 captured.

Professions: Cap extended from 60 → 100. Diplomat unavailable. New seasonal skills: Sandworm Traps, Summon Mummies, Nature's Touch, Trap Landmines.

Heroes / weapons: Scarlett upgradeable to UR (week 3). Exclusive weapons unlock: Marshall (Week 1), Schuyler (Week 3), McGregor (Week 6) via dedicated battle passes.

Radar Missions — Desert Ruins: 7-level progression with 2.016% odds of reaching final stage. Yields Merit Medals, resources, escape mini-games. Alliance Compasses unlock at Level 7.

Season 4 — Evernight Isle (Sakura Isle darkness)

Launch: Rolling per-server, ~6–7 weeks after S3 ends. Duration: 2-week pre-season + 8-week main + celebration week. Theme: Sakura Isle, a Japanese-inspired island paradise, plunged into endless darkness by the Nightfall Device and the release of the Oni Virus (ONV). Players must restore light.

Factions (introduced in S4 as a formal system):

  • Kage no Sato (Ninja Clan) — dark green aesthetic
  • Koubutai (Samurai Clan) — imperial red aesthetic

Warzones are grouped and choose sides in the Faction Award Event in Week 3; Copper Wars (Wed/Sat) occur across Weeks 4–7; Faction Capitol + War's Eve in Week 7.

Map: 13×13 grid (same as S3), 75×75 pixel squares.

  • 24 Level 1–6 cities (down from 40 in S3 — meaningfully denser competition)
  • 6 Digging Strongholds (Stone production 100–200/hour)
  • Trade Posts (Levels 1–4, unlock Week 3 Day 2)
  • 10 Sushi Restaurants (unlock progressively Weeks 1–4)

Signature building: The Lighthouse. Central power system storing electricity and pushing back darkness. Requires 4 zombie-powered generators (Optoelectronic Lab levels 1, 3, 10, 15), each operated by an Electrician. Features:

  • 4 brightness levels (L1–L4)
  • Power management system
  • Auto-adjustment toggle for conservation

Optoelectronic Lab (max L35) — increases Virus Resistance and unlocks generator repairs at specific thresholds. Analogous to S3's Curse Research Lab.

Blood Night Descend — the S4 flagship recurring PvE-into-PvP event. Occurs 3 times daily at 02:30, 10:30, and 18:30 server time, each lasting 30 minutes. Power consumption jumps to +18,000/minute during Blood Night. Four stages:

  • Stage 1: Oni Generals spawn; defeating them unlocks city buffs
  • Stage 2: Wandering Oniwagons appear (max 5 consecutive battle rounds)
  • Stage 3: Escalated difficulty
  • Stage 4: Holy Mountain event

Copper Wars — the main PvP event. Alliances declare on enemy Alliance Centers. Key rules change from S3: only 3 secondary buildings per Alliance Center; declaration range narrows weekly; minimum 11-tile spacing between Alliance Centers.

Maneki-neko discoveries: Similar to S3 desert artifacts; discovered using flares/base lights (Large Maneki-neko) or Electricians on nighttime expeditions (Mini Maneki-neko — 1 per day max). 3 daily claims (max 15).

Trade Posts contests run Tuesdays and Fridays at 12:00 server time; during Blood Night, governors can call additional caravans with Magatama-exclusive items.

City capture: 2 declarations/day, max 6 captured cities + 6 Digging Strongholds. Buffs unlock via Oni General defeats.

Heroes / weapons: Sarah upgrades to UR (Week 3 Day 1). Exclusive weapons: Lucius (Week 1), Adam (Week 3), Williams (Week 6).

Professions: Level cap remains 100. Diplomat unavailable. Tactics Cards debut here as a new equipable layer.

T11 troops unlock at season end during the celebration/off-season week.

Max plunder per round: 15% of defender's Copper. Multiple attackers share the plunder proportionally by damage inflicted.

Season 5 — Wild West / Golden Wasteland

Launch: Rolling per-server. Community timeline places Season 5 launch in early 2026 (before S6 launched April 13, 2026). MedievalFun's preview article (Nov 7, 2025) said Season 5 arrives "in 11 days," implying ~November 18, 2025 as first server rollout. Duration: 2-week pre-season + 8 weeks main. Retains celebration week pattern. Theme: Wild West / Golden Wasteland / Land of Liberty. Venom is the featured hero.

The structural break: single-warzone → 8-warzone shared map. S5 introduced "Octagon Clash": 8 warzones from 8 different servers converge onto a single battlefield. Map is 9× larger than previous seasons, plus a central area. This foreshadows and enables the S6 faction model.

New seasonal resources: | Resource | Purpose | Source | |---|---|---| | CrystalGold | Primary ranking currency, deposited in Banks for interest | City passive income, Bank deposits, Doom Elite first-kills, High Noon (max 15,000/day from Week 3), Trade Train whiskey trades (Week 2+) | | Coffee / Coffee Beans | Fuels Coffee Factory and Caffeine Institute upgrades | Coffee Factory production | | Whiskey | Trade Train consignment (converts to CrystalGold) | Trade Train |

Signature mechanic: Bank Strongholds.

  • Alliances capture 2 Bank Strongholds/day
  • Default max holding: 4 banks; increases +1 per city controlled up to 12 max
  • 3 CrystalGold deposits/day, up to 15 simultaneous 5-day deposits (interest-bearing)
  • Levels 1–6 available Week 1; Levels 7–10 open Week 4 with Virus Resistance requirements reaching 39,900
  • Sundays are Truce Day — no Bank Strongholds can be taken

Trade Train: A train continuously travels across the continent, stopping at multiple stations. Players consign Whiskey for CrystalGold profit — or raid competitors' trades for rewards. Introduces cross-alliance economic warfare not previously in the game.

War timing overhaul — the biggest quality-of-life change since S2. Previous seasons ran 24/7 combat. S5 introduced:

  • 3 fixed war time slots per day (1 hour each, spaced 7 hours apart)
  • Alliance Safe Time — alliances disable 1 of the 3 daily war slots for 7–15 consecutive hours of immunity (president-managed)
  • Cities can only be captured on war days: Wednesdays and Saturdays (2 captures per war day = 4/week max)

New seasonal buildings:

  • Caffeine Institute — Virus Resistance + coffee recipes
  • Coffee Factories — Coffee production
  • Protector's Field — houses special combat units (continues from S3)
  • Bars — Tank/Air/Missile hero damage buffs

Faction system: eliminated. Unlike S4 and (later) S6, S5 has no factions. Influence Points competition is free-for-all between all alliances across the 8 warzones.

Heroes / weapons:

  • Venom upgrades to UR
  • Mason, Violet, Scarlett, Sarah can also upgrade to UR with available items (returning heroes get "second-chance" ascension in S5)
  • Exclusive weapons: Fiona (Week 1), Stetmann (Week 3), Morrison (Week 6)
  • 4 heroes gain +10% monster damage: Venom, Fiona, Stetmann, Morrison

Pre-season structure (retained from S4):

  • Week 1: Season Preview (7 daily reveals), Season Boost preview, Faction Awards
  • Week 2: Gold Prospecting mini-game (Mon–Fri) → top 100 players/warzone determine Area Selection priority → Area Selection Tue–Sat (warzones choose spawn locations, higher rank = better area, presidents can swap positions until Saturday lock) → Capitol Conquest Day 6 (all alliances compete for Capitol without adjacency)

Season pass — Tycoon's Mansion HQ skin grants +5% Attack, plus HP/Defense bonuses.

Season 6 — Shadow Rainforest / Lost Rainforest

Launch: April 13, 2026 (first server rollout; rolling thereafter). Pre-season went live late March 2026. Duration: 2-week pre-season + 8 weeks main + celebration week. Theme: The Shadow Rainforest, home of three ancient clans — the Great River (their lifeline) has been poisoned by a spreading virus.

S6 is the biggest structural overhaul the game has shipped to date. Community sources uniformly rank it above any prior season in mechanical complexity.

Three clans (two playable + one neutral):

  • Deepwood Clan (green stag totem) — forest, "hunting and combat," defensive/resource-focused
  • Wetland Clan (blue crocodile totem) — swamp, "survival and resource control," aggressive/city-capturing
  • Great River Clan (neutral) — controls the central zone including the Ancestral Temple (the Capitol)

Map: Shadow Rainforest Mega Map = 8 warzones + 1 central area, 100% mirror-symmetrical for fair start. Warzones are split 4-vs-4 between factions. Environmental fog reduces visibility (shared among nearby allies, so clustered alliances have an advantage over spread-out ones).

Faction assignment: Pre-season Week 1 auto-groups 8 warzones into 2 groups of 4. Strongest warzone in each group = Faction Leader. Coin toss between leaders determines Deepwood vs Wetland — locked for the season.

Signature mechanic #1: Permanent City Destruction. In prior seasons, captured cities could be reclaimed. In S6, captured enemy-faction cities are automatically destroyed and cannot be rebuilt for the entire season. Impact on faction Influence Points:

  • Attacking faction: +50% of city's IP value
  • Defending faction: –100% of city's IP value (net 150% swing per destruction)
  • Destroyed cities do not count toward the attacker's city cap

Signature mechanic #2: Altars (Central Zone objectives). 5 Altars in the Great River central zone, each granting different Alliance Skills tied to a leadership role:

All Awakening Shards must now be obtained through in-game gameplay (Battle Pass rewards, Awakening Trial, Global Expedition) [LDShop Hero Awakening Guide, 2026-07-15]. The broader "pack removal" wave in the same window also stripped the $20/$50/$100 pop-up bundles and Custom Weekly Passes, replaced by a compensation package including 200 Exclusive Weapon Shards + 1,000 Valor Badges to every player's inbox [LDShop Update Guide via search snippet, 2026-07-15]. Stated goal per developer messaging: slow the hyper-competitive whale-driven power spike and shift toward season-pass style monetization.

Additional S6 mechanics:

  • Alliance Pacts — 7-day non-aggression treaties between same-faction alliances. Prevent City Destruction. Share adjacency benefits and open joint communication channels for up to 200 members. Make faction selection load-bearing.
  • Faction Technology — faction-wide research tree unlocked via Stronghold donations, applies buffs to all faction members after individual contribution thresholds met.
  • War Merit economy — earned via kills, captures, reinforcements, donations, rankings. Tiers Bronze → Elite with corresponding daily stipends and stat bonuses. War Merit Shop trades.
  • Fungus Institute (max ~L60) — enhances Virus Resistance (analog to Optoelectronic Lab / Curse Research Lab).
  • Spore Factory (max L30) — produces Spores.
  • Sanctuary timer buff: doubled from 10 → 20 minutes (occupies 1/3 of the 60-minute attack window). Sources call this a "stealth change" that significantly restricts strategic flexibility.
  • Alliance Skill cooldowns: 3–6 days.
  • Card meta shift: the popular "4+1 hybrid squad strategy" was removed from Global Expedition, forcing pure Tank / Missile / Aircraft formations backed by attribute-specific Tactics Cards.
  • Beneath the Ruins (added 2026-07-15) — Season 6's dedicated minigame, launched in TWO phases [Cpt Hedgehog Beneath the Ruins, 2026-07-15; TheGamesEdge Beneath the Ruins Guide, 2026-07-15]:
  • Solo Mode (Week 1 Day 2+): descending-platformer challenge. Guide your character downward through an ever-deepening stage, avoiding hazards. 1 new stage unlocks per day. Missed days stack — play back-to-back to catch up. Recurring cross-season patterns worth noting

Seasonal Resistance Stat rotation — every season since S3 has introduced a season-specific resistance stat that gates PvE damage:

  • S3 = Curse Resistance (Curse Research Lab)
  • S4 = Virus Resistance (Optoelectronic Lab)
  • S5 = Virus Resistance continued, requirement scales to 39,900 for high-tier Banks (Caffeine Institute)
  • S6 = Virus Resistance (Fungus Institute, max ~L60)

Season-specific building trinity — every season ships (a) a "research lab" for the resistance stat, (b) a producer for the shop currency, and (c) a producer for the ranking currency. Learn the pattern once, apply to any season.

Season-specific troop tier gate — T-troop upgrades unlock at season-end celebration weeks. T10 unlocked in S1, T11 in S4, T12 anticipated but unsourced.

Season-specific hero UR ascension — one designated SSR hero per season gets promoted to UR:

  • S3 = Scarlett
  • S4 = Sarah
  • S5 = Venom (plus retro UR paths for Mason/Violet/Scarlett/Sarah)
  • S6 = Braz (SSR→UR); plus Hero Awakening rotation for Kimberly/D.Va/Tesla

Exclusive Weapons rollout — every season ships 3 exclusive weapons on Weeks 1/3/6 via dedicated 7-day battle passes. Named-shard-only for activation, universal shards for upgrades. Since S6, this coexists with the Hero Awakening system.

Pre-season Capitol Conquest — introduced in S4, retained through S6: all alliances compete for Capitol without adjacency requirements on Week 2 Day 6 of pre-season. Winners maintain possession through the pre-season into the main season.

Alliance Safe Time — introduced in S5 (7–15 hours immunity), retained in S6. This is a permanent QoL shift; unlikely to be removed in S7+.

S3 week-by-week milestone timeline

Every week ships a set of missions on specific server-days. Each mission carries a warzone-collective reward chest goal that must be met before individual objectives count. City/building unlocks are staggered inside each week, not front-loaded on Day 1.

Week 1 — build-out & first PvE. [lastwartutorial S3 W1, 2026-07-15]

  • Day 1: Protector's Field, Curse Research Lab, and Blessing Fountain unlock. Purge Action (7-day zombie-killing leaderboard) and Digging Stronghold Clash (13-day) launch. Sandworm Crisis 14-day event launches. Recommendation from guides: level Protector's Field only to 10 initially — levels 11+ demand Mithril and Sacred Water needed elsewhere.
  • Day 2: Alliance Center unlocks; R4/R5 initiate repairs.
  • Day 3 + 12h: Level 1 cities unlock for City Clash.
  • Day 4: Marshall Exclusive Weapon 7-day battle pass opens (Tier-5 Marshall required to equip; 70 shards in pass, 50 needed for activation).
  • Day 3: Build an Oasis event begins — a 48-day long-term event running to end of season.
  • Day 6 + 12h: Level 2 cities unlock. [lastwartutorial S3 W1, 2026-07-15]

Week 2 — economy & first cross-warzone contact. [lastwartutorial S3 W2, 2026-07-15]

  • Day 1: Oasis Project greenification launches (individual leaderboard + warzone chest at 100,000 tiles). Archaeology & Digging (Desert Vaults) launches, runs through Week 8 — Archaeologist Hammers break stones for relics, "Battleship-style" (you don't need to break all stones). Alliance Vaults require cooperative help; daily limit on assisting allies. Warzone chest = donate 5M coins. Return of the Dead 19-day event begins — Desert Bosses attack Alliance Centers every Tuesday and Friday at 11:30 server time; 30 waves of mummies at 1-minute intervals (~30+ min). Failure damages AC; zero durability = no future rewards. Warzone chest = convert 100k units by helping allies.
  • Day 2: Hero Swap Event (2 tickets to swap XP + stars between UR heroes; Wall of Honor unchanged).
  • Day 3 + 12h: Level 3 cities ("Town") unlock. Warzone chest = capture 10 Digging Strongholds.
  • Day 6 + 12h: Level 4 cities ("Temple of the Sun") unlock. Warzone chest = kill 100k enemy units from opposing warzones.
  • Day 7 + 12h: Trade Posts unlock (cross-warzone raids begin — Trade Posts are individual captures, not alliance-wide). Warzone chest = kill 2,000 Doom Elites. [lastwartutorial S3 W2, 2026-07-15]

Week 3 — Scarlett UR window opens; final city tiers. [lastwartutorial S3 W3, 2026-07-15]

  • Day 1: Faction Awards resolve — the two strongest warzones become leaders of the two factions (Scarlet Legion / Golden Tribe). Warzone chest = 100k units via helping allies. Scarlett UR promotion mission opens — warzone chest requires donating 5,000,000 coins. Worm Expert daily-ranking event begins (Trap Landmines + damage vs. sandworms). Warzone chest = 2,000 Doom Elites.
  • Day 3: Level 5 cities ("Ancient Tombs") unlock. Warzone chest = 2,000 Doom Elites.
  • Day 4: Schuyler Exclusive Weapon battle pass opens (Tier-5 Schuyler required; 70 shards / 50 for activation).
  • Day 6: Level 6 cities ("Square of Judgment") unlock — highest city tier. Warzone chest = 100k enemy units from rival warzones. [lastwartutorial S3 W3, 2026-07-15]

Scarlett UR ascension requirements (Day 1 Week 3): Scarlett at 5 stars + Legendary Hero Badge. The Legendary Hero Badge is only awarded at Curse Research Lab L30 — this is why every guide flags CRL L30 as the highest single-building priority in the season. Scarlett SSR shards themselves also come from Individual Goals tied to CRL levels: 10 shards from L2–L19, 20 shards from L21–L24, 30 shards from L26–L29. [medievalfun Scarlett UR, 2026-07-15]

Week 4 — Spice Wars begin; Pyramid War arrives. [lastwartutorial S3 W4 + medievalfun Spice War, 2026-07-15]

  • Day 1: Oasis Project weekly reset (100k tiles warzone). Spice Wars begin — first Wed/Sat battle windows this week. Warzone chest = convert 100k units via helping allies. Spice War mechanic parallels Rare Soil War from earlier seasons.
  • Day 6: Pyramid War begins (Capitol / Great Pyramid becomes contestable through City Clash mechanics). Mission goal delayed 36h (Day 7 + 12h server time). Warzone chest = donate 5M coins collectively.
  • Additional: Overlord Growth Handbook pass opens Day 1 Week 4 in the Mall. [lastwartutorial S3 W4, 2026-07-15]

Weeks 5–6 — sustained Spice Wars + Altar upgrades. Spice Wars continue on Wed/Sat cadence. Oasis Project greenification transitions from city-perimeter-only (Weeks 1–4) to free expansion in Weeks 5–7 once regions cross 80% thresholds. [lastwar-guide S3, 2026-07-15]

Week 6: McGregor Exclusive Weapon battle pass opens (per S3 overview in main section above).

Week 7 — Faction Duel: 6 days of Invasion Right + Capitol War Day 7. [lastwarhandbook S3 Spice/Faction summary via search, 2026-07-15]

  • Days 1–6: Invasion Right contest — both factions accrue points across every event. The faction with more Invasion Right at end of Day 6 gains attack initiative at Capitol War.
  • Day 7 (Sunday, 12:00 server time): Capitol War. Capitol control = +10M Spice. Each Cannon controlled = +5M Spice. Season-defining battle. All warzones lock to the Capitol and their designated Strategic Area once battle begins.

Week 8 — final Spice Wars + settlement. Final Wed/Sat Spice War rounds. Season rankings finalize based on total Spice. Rewards distributed. Off-season / celebration transition begins.

S3 Spice War full mechanic — 4 phases

Wed & Sat, Weeks 4–7. Factions are set as Scarlet Legion (Red) vs Golden Tribe (Blue) based on Weeks 1–3 performance. Factions alternate attacker/defender roles across battles. [medievalfun Spice War, 2026-07-15]

  1. Declaration Stage — attacking alliance R4s select defending opponents in their ranking group. Each defender faces up to 3 attackers; defender may invite up to 2 allied reinforcements.
  2. Invitation Stage — defending alliance recruits backup; reinforcement alliances vote to accept via R3 members.
  3. Preparation Stage — final organization; no strategy changes permitted afterwards.
  4. War Stage — combat. Attackers must destroy the defending Alliance Center before timer expires to plunder Spice. Defenders win if AC survives.

Spice War economics:

  • Alliance Center destroyed = attacker takes 10% of defender's Spice.
  • Each secondary building destroyed = attacker takes 5% (4 pillars: Sacred Water Hut, Exploratory Well, Mithril Plant, Obelisk).
  • Cap: 30% maximum per Spice War (destroy AC + 4 pillars = 10 + 4×5 = 30%). This is the origin of the "30%" number quoted in the main S3 section above.
  • Defending alliances with fewer participants receive up to 3× reward multiplier — this incentivizes defending against overwhelming odds. [medievalfun Spice War, 2026-07-15]
S3 Desert Protector deep-dive

Three acquisition methods (in addition to "10% of battlefield losses convert via Recon Plane assist"): [medievalfun Protectors + lastwartutorial S3, 2026-07-15]

  1. Alliance Support — allies send Recon Planes to speed conversions of lost units.
  2. Protector Horns from Desert Bazaar — 90 Sandworm Scales = 300 horns/units, weekly limit. Paid-currency lever for topping up Protector reserves before big fights.
  3. Profession skill "Protector's Oath" at Level 50 — grants instant Desert Protectors (no combat losses required).

Indomitable Will profession skill at Level 70 restores up to 10% of Protector losses post-combat (the only recovery mechanic — otherwise Protectors are single-use). [lastwartutorial S3 + medievalfun, 2026-07-15]

Protector Power: Protectors are cited as ~10× stronger than regular Season 3 troops at equivalent unit level. [medievalfun Protectors, 2026-07-15]

Skill unlock thresholds (community-guide detail): 500+ = +25% ATK/DEF/HP + 250 Curse Resistance; 750+ = Death Shockwave (killed Protectors damage attackers); 1000+ = Blue Flame Spell (winning attacks debuff enemy base for 1h — guides flag its actual battlefield effect as "unclear if functional"); 1250+ = Protector Rally (1-minute countdown; leader buff inheritance for all rally members; 3× march speed). [medievalfun Protectors, 2026-07-15]

Meta rule: Protectors are for high-value engagements only — Digging Stronghold captures, Spice Wars, Trade Wars, and Giant Sandworms. Burning them on generic PvE or low-level city captures is the single most-cited waste in community guides. [lastwarhandbook S3 via search, 2026-07-15]

S3 Sandworm reward economy

Three sizes: Small (solo-defeatable, wraps base if you're the last alliance member to activate), Large (~35M power per rally, can teleport bases outside AC range), Giant (server-wide discovery, ~48M power per rally, "insane" rewards). [medievalfun Sandworms, 2026-07-15]

No Curse Resistance is required for any sandworm size — sandworms are the one PvE content that ignores the CRL gate. [lastwartutorial S3 via search, 2026-07-15]

Finder's Reward > Kill Reward — the highest-leverage Sandworm mechanic:

  • Giant Sandworm Finder's Reward: 500 Sandworm Scales + 10 Hero's Return Recruitment tickets.
  • Giant Sandworm Defeat Reward: 20 Sandworm Scales.
  • 25× differential favors the finder. [medievalfun Sandworms, 2026-07-15]

Discovery methods:

  1. Tap the question-mark icon when fully charged (last person in alliance to click gets the finder bonus).
  2. Deploy Trap Landmine (profession Level 65) on Sandworm paths visible on the world map.

Critical mistakes to avoid:

  • Do NOT teleport your base when wrapped by a small Sandworm — this eliminates rewards for you AND everyone else in your alliance.
  • Position base inside Alliance Center range before Large Sandworm spawns (Large Sandworms teleport bases outside AC range as their signature disruption). [medievalfun Sandworms, 2026-07-15]
S3 community meta strategies & common mistakes

Consolidated from MedievalFun's "10 Key Tips" and cross-referenced with lastwar-guide.org / lastwartutorial / earnne. [medievalfun S3 tips + multi-source, 2026-07-15]

Pre-season prep (Weeks -3 to 0):

  • Save stamina for at least 3 weeks before Season 3. Minimize stamina-consuming activities so you can front-load greenification and seasonal contributions when the map opens.

Early-game (Weeks 1–2):

  • Stay inside Alliance Center range at all times. Bases outside AC range get random Sandworm-triggered teleports that scatter you across the map — costly to recover from.
  • Kill Wandering Mummy Warlords daily — best Mithril source in the early game, and no Curse Resistance is required to fight them.
  • Level Curse Research Lab first, Protector's Field second. Cap PF at L10 until CRL is at L30. New players commonly over-invest in Protector's Field because it produces the flashy signature unit, but Protectors without Curse Resistance can't survive high-tier PvE.
  • Visit Alliance Center 2×/day for the resource bubble refresh (Mithril, Sacred Water, Oil).

Greenification/economy:

  • Unlock Double Greenification at profession skill level 35 — covers 3 tiles per action instead of 1, and finds Desert Artifacts.
  • Don't neglect the 100k-tile weekly warzone chest — one of the easiest chests to miss, and skipping it starves the whole warzone of the tier-reward.

Mid-game (Weeks 3–4):

  • Time hero promotions for Thursday's "Training Heroes" day — event scoring stacks with normal progression. Common mistake: promoting Scarlett to UR the moment Week 3 Day 1 opens instead of waiting until Thursday.
  • Complete Desert Treasure Vaults for 400k Mithril + exclusive weapon shards — Alliance Vaults require coordinated help but yield 4-5× more than solo vaults.

Spice Wars (Weeks 4–7):

  • Defend the Alliance Center as top priority — a destroyed AC costs 10% of your Spice per lost war, cascading with pillar destruction to the 30% cap.
  • Attackers: teleport near defender's AC and use Warlord Missiles to clear space before rallying.
  • Defenders: rotate reinforcements steadily — heal troops during lulls, don't burn everything in the first wave.
  • Anchor weekly planning around Spice War Wednesday and Saturday — every other event's timing should support your Spice War posture.

Hero investment priority: Scarlett, Marshall, Schuyler, McGregor — season-3-specific damage bonuses vs. monsters/sandworms. [medievalfun S3 tips, 2026-07-15]

Trade Post discounts (from Week 2): Daily deals on speedups, recruitment tickets, and traps. Underused by most players because Trade Posts are individually captured (not alliance-wide), so many players never engage. [medievalfun S3 tips, 2026-07-15]
