TL;DR

Season 2 (marketed as Polar Storm, sometimes Arctic Storm or Frozen) is Last War: Survival's second seasonal ruleset, replacing Season 1's virus-stack mechanic with an active per-tile temperature system. The map is a frozen concentric grid running from -15 °C at the outer corners to -80 °C at the Capitol, with per-band drops of roughly 15 °C per zone; if a base's temperature drops below -20 °C for 5 minutes it enters the Frozen state, which disables rally, teleport, and relocation (see 04-squads-combat for rally mechanics and 08-alliance-systems for hive coordination implications). Survival is driven by three new buildings — the personal High-Heat Furnace (level 30 max, coal-burning, adds +5.5 °C to +20 °C activation / +8 °C to +30 °C overdrive), the shared Alliance Furnace (level 20 max, R4/R5 deployed on Day 1, produces +3 °C to +12.5 °C normal / up to +25 °C overdrive across a shared radius), and the Titanium Alloy Plant (level 30 max, +720 titanium/hr per level, up to 4 factories organically + 1 from weekly pass). Territory is captured for Rare Soil (the seasonal ranking currency) — max 6 cities + 4 dig sites per alliance, 2 captures per day, 36-hour post-capture protection, city temperature bonus scaling +5 °C at L1 → +60 °C at L6 — culminating in the Rare Soil War in Week 4 where factions can plunder 20–30 % of enemy soil stocks under a -70 °C blizzard.

Core mechanics

The temperature system

Season 2 abandons the flat "virus stack" debuff of Season 1 in favor of a coordinate-based ambient temperature. Every tile on the map has an inherent temperature that is a function of its distance from the map center (the Capitol / Nuclear Furnace). A Thermal Map toggle in the UI paints the base land temperature as a heatmap overlay — critical for pre-planning hive placement because that overlay does not account for city furnaces, the Nuclear Furnace, blizzards, or any dynamic heat modifier; it reveals only the raw geographic baseline. [Handbook Temperature Guide, lastwartutorial.com]

The map is divided into seven concentric zones, each a fixed ambient band: -15, -30, -40, -50, -60, -70, -80 °C, moving inward. The corners of the map are the warmest and safest ground; the very center where the Capitol sits is the coldest and most unlivable position without stacked heat sources. [lastwar-tutorial.com, lastwartutorial.com]

Each base has its own current base temperature, computed as ambient_tile_temperature + sum(active_heat_bonuses). Heat sources stack additively. When the resulting number drops below the freeze threshold, the base enters a countdown.

Freezing threshold and Frozen state

The universal freeze threshold is -20 °C. If a base's current temperature stays below -20 °C for a continuous 5-minute countdown, the base transitions into the Frozen state. [multiple sources: theriagames.com, lastwar-guide.org, medievalfun.com, lastwartutorial.com]

While Frozen, the following actions become unavailable:

  • Rally — cannot start rallies, cannot join in-flight rallies. If a base freezes mid-rally-sequence during a capture attempt, it stops contributing until it thaws.
  • Teleport — no random, targeted, alliance-formation, or cross-server teleports.
  • Relocation — the base cannot move on the map.
  • Shield — inside missile-created "Contaminated Land" zones, shields cannot be activated (see Warlord Missile below).

Production, healing, and troop-morale debuffs are also applied while cold (bluestacks.com), though the exact scaling has not been surfaced in a single authoritative source.

Defrost / thawing

To escape Frozen state, the base's total temperature must rise above the freeze threshold and hold for 5 minutes (mirroring the freeze countdown). The primary defrost methods are:

  1. Fire your own High-Heat Furnace (or flip it to overdrive) so your local heat output overrides the ambient chill.
  2. Ally spy plane — an alliance-mate can fly a spy plane over your base with heating enabled to raise your local temperature and initiate defrost. This is the primary rescue method when a member's own coal has run out. [lastwartutorial.com search snippet, lastwar-tutorial.com]
  3. Move within range of an active Alliance Furnace or captured city — passive but slow because relocation itself is disabled while Frozen. In practice this only works if the base was pre-positioned before it froze.

A "safe ally heating limit" of roughly +38 °C is referenced as the cutoff before fire damage risk begins around +40 °C, meaning heating too aggressively can flip from freeze protection into over-heat penalty. [lastwar-guide.org Week 4 guide]

Blizzards

Blizzards are temporary map-wide temperature drops that apply on top of the baseline gradient, announced via a top-left notification hours in advance so alliances can prepare. Each blizzard event lasts 3 hours. The per-week severity climbs sharply:

WeekBlizzard dropEffective floor near center
1–2-30 °C≈ -110 °C
3-50 °C≈ -130 °C
4+-70 °C≈ -150 °C

[Handbook Temperature Guide, lastwartutorial.com]

The Week 4 -70 °C blizzard runs concurrently with the opening of the Rare Soil War — this is the most dangerous single event in the season and dictates most alliances' furnace budgets. [lastwar-guide.org]

The High-Heat Furnace exposes an "auto-activate in blizzard" toggle that automatically switches the furnace on (and to overdrive if configured) when a blizzard begins. Recommended to enable Day 1. [lastwarguide.net]

The three seasonal buildings

Season 2 introduces three new buildings that dominate the early game.

Day-1 landing mechanics

When Season 2 begins, every alliance is teleported as a hive to a corner (or edge) of the map in a pre-arranged formation. The map transition happens on a scheduled server rotation (community reports place this around 13:00 server time for many warzones); resources dropped from Doom Walker "first kill" bonuses only reward seasonal Coal after the map has transitioned to winter. [dcinside minor gallery, medievalfun.com]

The critical Day-1 priority list is essentially universal across guides:

  1. Reposition the hive if the drop location is too crowded or too close to hostile alliances. The whole hive can be moved using the free alliance-relocation trick (leaders shift the Assembly Point; members teleport back and forth without spending personal teleports). [lastwar-tutorial.com teleporter guide]
  2. Place the Alliance Furnace in the center of the hive on Level-1 territory.
  3. Garrison the Alliance Furnace with as many players as possible to burn through the ~20 reinforcements gate quickly.
  4. Every member builds their own High-Heat Furnace and Titanium Alloy Factory #1 and enables the auto-blizzard toggle.
  5. Weaker players park closer to the Alliance Furnace; stronger players anchor the outer ring so they can absorb hits and provide reinforcement.
  6. Do not kill the highest-level Wanderer / Doom Walker before the map transition — the coal reward only pays out post-transition. Community reports a ~200,000 coal payout for a level 180 Wanderer first-kill after transition. [dcinside gall/12212]
  7. Stack radar missions before the season opens so they can be cleared on Day 1 for guaranteed coal rewards. [lastwartutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide]
Cities and dig sites

The map is dotted with two capturable territory types, both of which are essential:

Cities (도시 / 마을 / 城)

  • Provide passive heat bonus to their owner and any base parked within their heat radius: L1 = +5 °C → L6 = +60 °C in +11 °C increments. [lastwartutorial.com]
  • Produce Rare Soil at rates from 350/hr (L1) to 1,000/hr (L6).
  • Newly captured cities snap their tile temperature up to -10 °C (a warm island in the frost). [WebSearch: Season 2 city capture rules]
  • Max 6 cities per alliance, max 2 captured per day, ~6 days protection after capture. [lastwarguide.net]
  • Unlock schedule:
  • L1 (Village): Week 1 day 3 + 12h
  • L2 (Town): Week 1 day 6 + 12h
  • L3: Week 2 day 3 + 12h
  • L4: Week 2 day 6 + 12h
  • L5: Week 3 day 3 + 12h
  • L6: Week 3 day 6 + 12h
  • L7: Week 4 day 7 + 12h (Capitol-tier / rare)
  • Only Level 6 cities enable Capitol fighting. [lastwar-tutorial.com]

Dig Sites (채굴장 / 광산 / mining sites)

  • Produce Coal and Rare Soil. Coal output: L1 = 2,736/hr → L6 = 3,456/hr. Rare Soil output: 100–150/hr at L1–6. [lastwartutorial.com]
  • Max 4 dig sites per alliance (independent of city count).
  • Max 2 captured per day, 36-hour protection post-capture.
  • Guarded by mutant Polar Beasts that must be defeated first. Beast types have rock-paper-scissors weaknesses:
  • Gorilla (Aircraft type) → weak to Missile Vehicles
  • Polar Bear (Missile type) → weak to Tanks
  • Mammoth (Tank type) → weak to Aircraft
  • (Beast Crisis event uses slightly different naming: Polar Bear → Tanks, Ice Wolf → Aircraft, Ice Serpent → Missiles.)
  • Virus Resistance minimums by dig-site level: L1 = 4,000, L2 = 6,500, L3 = 8,500, L4 = 9,500, L5 = 10,000, L6 = 10,500.
  • Squad strength recommendation: 25 M+ for main assault.
  • Capture is competitive: the first alliance to reach 100 % occupation wins. If no alliance reaches 100 % within 4 hours, the attempt fails and everyone is refunded. [lootbar.com]

Adjacency rule: an alliance's first territory captured in the season must be a Level 1 Dig Site. From there, every subsequent capture must be adjacent to existing alliance territory — this drives the "expanding claim" pattern outward from the corner. [lastwartutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide]

Rare Soil — the season ranking currency

Rare Soil (희토 / 稀土 / "Rare Earth") replaces the Season-1 model where city control directly = ranking. In Season 2, control matters only insofar as it produces Rare Soil, which stockpiles in the Alliance Capitol and drives both:

  1. Alliance Furnace auto-upgrades (stockpile thresholds trigger level-ups).
  2. Season-end alliance rank (which alliance has the most stockpiled soil).

Cities and dig sites are consequently valued by their soil production rate, not their raw coordinates.

Hidden supply caches: each city has hidden supply caches that appear when the local ground temperature exceeds 0 °C. Players use incineration items (throwable heat bombs) to melt the ice and reveal them. Frozen supplies require spy-plane heating with 5 stamina per hit for 1,000 durability to defrost. Daily reclaim limit: 5 attempts. [lastwartutorial.com, dcinside gall/12212]

Rare Soil War (Week 4+)

Unlocked in Week 4, the Rare Soil War is a scheduled cross-alliance combat event whose objective is to destroy the enemy Alliance Furnace and plunder up to 20 % of their Rare Soil stockpile (30 % after the Showdown event later in the season). [lastwarguide.net, lastwartutorial.com]

20 % / 30 % plunder confirmed 2026-07-15 — third-source cross-check via lastwarguide.net/en/seasons/season-2 search excerpt ("The Alliance can steal up to 20% of the enemy's Rare Soil stock, or up to 30% during the Showdown"). Previously KB-flagged as single-sourced (Tier C only); now upgraded to two Tier B/C sources agreeing on identical percentages. The 30 % applies specifically during the Showdown week (Week 6 per lastwartutorial.com/season-2-week-6/), not the entire post-Week-4 window — an alliance mid-Season-2 rally planner should treat 20 % as the default expected transfer and 30 % as a Week-6 pulse-event modifier.

Faction system: at the "Faction Awards" event in Week 3, all warzones vote themselves into one of two factions — Rebels or Gendarmerie — with 8 warzones split 4-and-4. Only inter-faction attacks are allowed.

Ranking groups: alliances are split into 4 groups by Rare Soil stockpile — ranks 1–10, 11–20, 21–30, 31–50. Attacks are only allowed within the same group.

Two rounds per week, 5-stage cycle each:

  • 1v1, 2v2, 3v3 → 40 min

Warlord Missile: high-level attackers can launch a Warlord Missile at an enemy Alliance Furnace:

  • 3-minute (180-second) preparation window after launch during which the Warlord's own base is revealed to the entire server via world-chat broadcast and the Warlord enters "War Fever" status. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15; lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]
  • Launch can be attempted during both the Preparation Stage and the Combat Stage of a war round, not only during the 30-minute Prep. [lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]
  • Impact zone: 25×25 tile grid ("Contaminated Land") in Season 2 that lasts 1 hour, during which bases inside cannot activate shields. Furnace takes 30,000 durability damage on a successful impact. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15]
  • Season 3 blast size expansion (added 2026-07-16): Season 3 Golden Kingdom / Spice Wars / Faction Duel expands the same Warlord Missile mechanic to a 35×35 tile blast. Season 2 remains 25×25. Any downstream Warlord Missile simulator tool that spans multiple seasons must key blast size off the current season. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 3 Spice Wars & Faction Duel Complete Guide search-excerpt 2026-07-16 Tier B; direct fetch 403]
  • Multiple Warlords from allied attacker alliances can queue simultaneous missiles at the same furnace. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15]

Close-perimeter defender ping — the mechanic the KB was missing. On successful impact, every base inside the 25×25 blast area is forcibly relocated to a random position on the map — except the Warlord's own base if it happens to be inside the radius. This is the "close-perimeter ping" observed in community reports: defenders reinforcing the Alliance Furnace do not merely lose their shield — the game physically evicts them from their reinforcement position, forcing the furnace to be re-reinforced under fire while defenders find their new (often deep-frozen) landing tiles, shield up, and teleport back. Reinforcement squads inside the furnace itself are not evicted (they are inside the building, not on a tile), but any base staged next to the furnace to launch counter-rallies is displaced. Shields are also blocked inside the Contaminated Land for the full 1-hour duration, so re-shielding at the random landing tile is possible but re-shielding at any tile inside the 25×25 zone is not. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15; lastwarguide.net Season 2, 2026-07-15]

Blizzard interaction with the ping. If the missile lands during an active blizzard, random-teleported bases can arrive on frozen tiles with insufficient personal heat and start the 5-minute freeze countdown immediately at their new location — a compounding penalty that removes them from the war entirely. [lastwar-guide.org Season 2 Week 4, 2026-07-15]

Intercept play — cancelling the missile before it fires. During the 3-minute prep window the Warlord's coordinates are public and defenders on the enemy server can rally-attack the Warlord's base. The cancel condition is precisely: the Warlord's base moves (relocates OR is breached during the 3-minute window) → missile launch fails → 72-hour cooldown on the Warlord's missile ability. A "breach" appears to mean any successful hit that forces base displacement or damages it enough that the Warlord relocates defensively; the surveyed sources do not publish a fixed damage number to trigger the cancel. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15; lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]

"Failed launch" definition (specific to the 72-hour cooldown). A failed launch is any of:

  1. Warlord's base relocates during the 3-minute prep window (whether voluntarily to dodge or involuntarily via breach).
  2. Warlord moves off the target server before impact (Warlord must remain on the target furnace's server and within its catchment area). [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15]

A cancel triggered by rally-breach counts as a failed launch and applies the 72-hour cooldown. The 72-hour cooldown is on the missile skill, not the Warlord role — the alliance's Warlord officer can still be replaced by another R4 with the Warlord title, but that alternate must not have burned their own missile skill recently. This creates the standard "shadow Warlord" pattern where alliances rotate Warlord titles between multiple R4s to keep at least one missile skill available per war round. [inferred from lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War + 08-alliance-systems R4 title mechanics; needs in-game confirmation]

Defensive positioning consequence. Because the 25×25 ping evicts everyone in the blast area, the standard defense guidance is: park every base at least 5 tiles from the Alliance Furnace tile (outside the 25×25 blast when centered on the furnace, which corresponds to roughly a ring 5-12 tiles out depending on furnace footprint). The Assembly Point should be placed near the furnace but outside the missile blast radius so evicted defenders have a reliable return tile to shield up and teleport back from. [lastwar-guide.org Season 2 Week 4, 2026-07-15]

Plunder distribution: multi-attacker splits are proportional to furnace-durability damage dealt.

Rare Soil War — timing conventions (when does the war round actually start?)

The Stage-3 (30-min Prep) and Stage-4 (20-40-min Combat) windows always fire on Wednesday and Saturday of the war week, but the exact clock hour at which they open is not a universal Season 2 constant — it is a per-warzone value driven by that warzone's assigned server-time offset. What surveyed sources confirm:

  1. Combat opens 12:30 server time on each war day (Wednesday round, Saturday round). The 30-minute Prep stage runs 12:00-12:30 server time, and Combat runs 12:30-13:10 server time (40 min max, less in imbalanced brackets). [lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]
  2. "Server time" = the warzone's fixed regional server-time offset, set when the warzone was created. It does not auto-adjust to member time zones. A community observation of "for us it was 16:00 UK time" reflects the fact that that warzone's server-time 12:30 mapped to 16:00 UTC+0 — i.e., server-time was UTC-3.5 or similar. Different warzones fire at different UK/PST/KST clock hours because different warzones sit on different server-time offsets. [community observation via r/LastWarSurvivalGame Season 2 threads — filed as Tier C]
  3. Warzone server-time offset is not R5-configurable in Season 2. Season 2 predates the Season 5 "Alliance Safe Time" feature that lets R5s choose one of three attack windows per war day. In Season 2, every alliance on a warzone fights on the same fixed 12:30-server-time schedule; there is no per-alliance safe window. The three-windows-per-war-day system is a Season 5+ mechanic and does not retroactively apply to Season 2 rules. [cpt-hedge.com Alliance Safe Time Guide (S5/S6), 2026-07-15; distinction is explicit in the source]
  4. Full round cadence per war week (repeated Weeks 4-end):
  • Monday — Stage 0 (Declaration Wait, 24h): rankings visible
  • Tuesday — Stage 1 (War Declaration, 24h): attackers pick targets
  • Wednesday 00:00-12:00 server time — Stage 2 (Invitation, 12h): defenders invite allied support
  • Wednesday 12:00-12:30 server time — Stage 3 (Preparation, 30 min): matchups locked, Warlord Missiles can be launched
  • Wednesday 12:30-13:10 server time (max) — Stage 4 (Combat): actual furnace assault
  • Wednesday 13:10 through Thursday reset — Stage 5 (End): rewards distributed
  • Thursday-Saturday: identical 5-stage cycle for the Saturday round

[lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15; lastwarguide.net Season 2, 2026-07-15]

  1. Regional server-time conventions. No surveyed source publishes a canonical warzone → server-time-offset table. Community-observed offsets in circulation (Tier C, uncorroborated):
  • KR warzones often server-time = KST (UTC+9), so Combat 12:30 = 12:30 KST = 03:30 UTC
  • NA warzones often server-time = EST/PST-adjacent, Combat 12:30 ≈ 17:30-20:30 UTC
  • EU warzones often server-time = CET-adjacent, Combat 12:30 ≈ 11:30-12:30 UTC
  • The one UK-reporting community example (Combat 12:30 server = 16:00 UK) is consistent with a UTC-3.5 warzone (LATAM or Atlantic). Do NOT publish these as authoritative — they need per-warzone verification.

[multiple r/LastWarSurvivalGame comments; filed as weakly-sourced in open-questions]

Full round duration. From the top of Stage 0 (Monday reset) to the end of Stage 5 (Thursday reset) is ~72 hours per round, of which the actual combat window is at most 40 minutes. Two rounds per week = ~144 hours of war-cycle scheduling with at most 80 minutes of live combat, which is why coordination and rally-timing external tooling matters far more than raw combat hours.

Rare Soil War — faction rotation schedule across the 4 war weeks

Rebels and Gendarmerie swap attacker/defender roles between Wednesday and Saturday of each war week, and the assignment flips again the following week. The rotation is not a single-alliance choice — it is set by the season for the entire faction:

War weekWednesdaySaturday
Week 4Rebels attack, Gendarmerie defendGendarmerie attack, Rebels defend
Week 5Gendarmerie attack, Rebels defendRebels attack, Gendarmerie defend
Week 6 (Showdown)Rebels attack, Gendarmerie defendGendarmerie attack, Rebels defend
Week 7Gendarmerie attack, Rebels defendRebels attack, Gendarmerie defend

[lastwarhandbook.com Rare Soil War Season 2 Guide, 2026-07-15 — search excerpt: "The gendarmerie is in the fourth week of the season on Wednesday Defender and on Saturday Attacker. In the fifth week of the season is the gendarmerie on Wednesday Attacker and on Saturday Defender."]

Implication for the alliance planner: every alliance fights exactly one attack round and one defense round per week in Weeks 4-7. The Warlord Missile budget must be planned across 4 attack days total (roughly 1 Warlord missile per week for the alliance's Warlord officer, respecting the 72-hour skill cooldown). Officer-title rotation to a shadow Warlord is required if the same officer wants both Wednesday and Saturday launches in a two-week span.

Rare Soil ranking — cumulative accumulation, not end-state hold

Confirmed 2026-07-15: Season-end alliance ranking is based on total Rare Soil stockpiled across the entire season, not on which alliance controls the most territory when the season ends. This is a fundamental change from Season 1 (where end-of-season city control = rank) and drives the "capture early, hold forever" F2P strategy:

"The ranking system rewards sustained accumulation, not peak military power. Capture territory early and hold it — a Level 2 dig site held from Week 1 accumulates more Rare Soil over 48 days than a high-level city captured in Week 6."
[lastwarhandbook.com Season 2 Guide, 2026-07-15 (via WebSearch excerpt)]

Consequences for hive planning:

  1. Week-1 speed matters more than late-season power spikes. An alliance that deploys its Alliance Furnace and captures its first L1 Dig Site within Hour 12 of Day 1 will out-accumulate an alliance that spends Week 1 optimizing before its first capture — even if the second alliance ends up with more combat power in Week 6.
  2. Rare Soil War plunder is worth less than steady-state production. A successful 20% plunder of an enemy's stockpile shifts soil between alliances but does not create new soil; over 8 weeks, the alliance that spends less time attacking and more time farming captured territory typically wins.
  3. Do not over-capture into a higher bracket you cannot defend. Rare Soil War brackets (Ranks 1-10, 11-20, 21-30, 31-50) are set by stockpile — capturing extra cities can promote you into a tougher bracket where you get plundered more than you can produce. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 2 Guide, 2026-07-15]
Week 5 endgame — Nuclear Furnace reactivation and Glacieradon boss cycle

The season's endgame is not the Rare Soil War — it is the Nuclear Furnace reactivation event that opens in Week 5. The Nuclear Furnace sits at the map's absolute center (Zone 7, ambient -80 °C) and is a warzone-scale cooperation event that runs concurrently with the -90 °C blizzard cycle. Every alliance on the warzone contributes to a shared 100% activation bar, and four Glacieradon bosses (one per map corner) march inward to sabotage that progress.

Cold Wave Alert -90 (Week 5, Day 1, 7 days). Blizzards intensify from Week 4's -70 °C to -90 °C. Warzone-collective goal: warm up allies 10,000 times to unlock the reward chest. [lastwartutorial.com Season 2 Week 5, 2026-07-15]

Nuclear Furnace activation (Week 5, Day 1, 7 days — but event start day varies by server: Monday, Tuesday, or Thursday observed). The mission:

  • Per-player contribution: send troops to the Nuclear Furnace via the green-truck icon on the furnace tile
  • Stamina cost: 5 stamina per contribution
  • Daily cap: 10 contributions per day per player
  • Warzone goal: reach 100% activation on the shared bar before season end
  • Progress erosion: each Glacieradon step lowers activation progress by ~1% every 4 hours while walking
  • Warzone-collective side goal: donate 1,000,000 coal to Alliance Furnaces across the warzone

[lastwartutorial.com Season 2 Week 5, 2026-07-15]

Glacieradon boss (4 spawns, 1 per map corner, over 4 consecutive days). Cannot be attacked while walking on open map — attackable only when passing through a city. This makes city-ownership timing a strategic asset separate from Rare Soil production. Mechanics:

  • Attack cost: 10 stamina per attack
  • Daily cap: 5 attacks per day per player per warzone
  • Contribution: alliance reinforcements to attacked cities reduce Glacieradon HP
  • Defense payoff: successful defense preserves city ownership (a Glacieradon walking through your city normally would push you off)

[lastwartutorial.com Season 2 Week 5, 2026-07-15]

Rewards distribution: Final rewards are based on how well your warzone progresses vs. other warzones — a cross-warzone leaderboard is implicit. Servers with poor Nuclear Furnace activation reap smaller rewards than servers that hit 100% early.

Nuclear Furnace as a Week-4 capturable objective. The Nuclear Furnace itself is unlocked for capture on Week 4, Day 6 + 12h (per Week 4 event schedule). This is separate from the Week 5 reactivation event — Week 4 is territory-claim, Week 5 is warzone-collective activation. [lastwartutorial.com Season 2 Week 4, 2026-07-15]

Season length reconciled — 8 weeks total, with 48 "core" days across weeks 1-7

The prior contradiction between the "48 days / 5 weeks" (theriagames.com) and "8 weeks" (lootbar.com) sources is resolved as of 2026-07-15: the season runs approximately 8 weeks total (some sources say 8-10 with warzone-specific tail variation), of which:

  • Weeks 1-3: ~21 days of setup, capture unlocks, faction voting
  • Weeks 4-7: ~28 days of active Rare Soil War (Wednesday + Saturday combat, 8 total war rounds), Nuclear Furnace reactivation event begins Week 5
  • Week 8 (and possibly 9-10): wind-down, final Glacieradon defeats, season-end rewards distribution

[lastwarvault.com Season 2 Timeline Guide, 2026-07-15 — search excerpt: "Season 2 runs roughly 8-10 weeks"; lastwar-guide.org Season 2 Guide, 2026-07-15]

The "48 days" figure corresponds to the first 7 weeks of core content (Wk 1-7 × 7 days ≈ 49); the "5 weeks" figure likely refers to the weeks with unique unlock content (Wk 1-5, before content settles into the repeating Wed/Sat war cycle). Both are correct measures of different things.

Teleportation rules in Season 2

Standard cross-server and in-map teleports work in Season 2 with three modifiers:

  1. Frozen state disables ALL teleport options. No random, targeted, cross-server, or alliance-formation teleport is possible while a base is Frozen. [multiple sources]
  2. Cross-server teleport is unlimited during combat events (Duel VS on Saturday, Capitol War, Season battles). [lastwar-tutorial.com]
  3. Free Season Grouping Teleport is available from Week 3 — accessed via Season 2 → Grouping Teleport in the season menu, this lets an entire alliance move to another server (and back) free of personal teleport charges. This is the vehicle for merged alliances and coordinated attacks. [lastwar-tutorial.com]
  4. Cooldown: returning from an enemy server triggers a 5-minute cooldown before the next hop.

The free alliance-relocation trick works in Season 2 exactly as in Season 1: the R5 shifts the alliance's Assembly Point, and members teleport back and forth without personal teleport cost, allowing the entire hive to move.

Because a single heat source is insufficient once you approach the Capitol, all sources stack additively on the base temperature:

Minimum survival stack without an Alliance Furnace in a Week 4 -70 °C blizzard: L30 furnace overdrive (+30) + L3 Tower of Victory (+5) + Intensive Overload (+6) = +41 °C bonus, still leaves you at ambient — 70 + 41 = -29 °C in the outer ring, which trips the -20 °C freeze if you're anywhere but the very corners. This is why the Alliance Furnace is not optional past Week 3. [heaven-guardian.com, lastwar-guide.org]

Rally mechanics use the leader's temperature bonus: when initiating a group attack, all rally members receive the leader's temperature bonus so that lower-power players can participate in operations deep in the frozen center. [dcinside gall/12212]

Open questions

  1. Alliance Furnace tile radius by level — every source describes the radius qualitatively ("wide", "expands with level", "boosted by season research") but none published a numeric tile-count-per-level table.   3. Alliance Furnace coal consumption per minute by level (L1–L19). Only L20 numbers (1,440 / 5,760) are cited.
  4. Rare Soil stockpile threshold for each Alliance Furnace auto-upgrade — no source lists the soil-count needed to progress L1→L2, L2→L3, etc.
  5. City heat-bonus radius — is the +5→+60 °C bonus applied only to the city tile itself, or a radius around it? Sources are ambiguous.
  6. Exact production/healing/morale debuff percentages applied while cold at different temperature bands. Called out as an effect but never quantified in surveyed sources.
  7. ~~Season length: 48 days / 5 weeks vs. 8 weeks?~~ RESOLVED 2026-07-15 — season is ~8 weeks total (8-10 with warzone tail variation). "48 days" = weeks 1-7 core content; "5 weeks" = weeks with unique unlock content before repeating Wed/Sat war cycle. See Season length reconciled subsection.
