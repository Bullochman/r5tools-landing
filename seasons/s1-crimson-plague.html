Release history (real-world dates)

Season 1 first went live on May 13, 2025 on server range S101-S196, per First Fun Group's official Facebook announcement (referenced in a "running for" community post at facebook.com/lastwarsurvival) [facebook.com/lastwarsurvival post 399609049726381, 2025-05-13, via search 2026-07-15 — Tier A official social post]. Subsequent server groups roll into Season 1 on a staggered schedule as newer warzones age past the ~90-100-day pre-season eligibility threshold; no consolidated public rollout calendar exists (filed to open-questions). The KB-derivable rule of thumb: any warzone whose founding date is >~120 days old is either in-Season-1 or has already progressed past it. See LW Atlas (lwatlas.com) and FarmOps (lastwar.farm) for live per-server Season status queries.

---

Terminology alert. Community shorthand — especially inside alliance chat and on YouTube — often calls Season 1 "Doomsday." That is misleading. The official season title is "The Crimson Plague." In-game, "Doomsday" is a separate, permanent, bi-weekly event (Doom Walker rally) that has nothing to do with the Season 1 map or ministries. The two overlap thematically (post-apocalyptic, viruses, "doom" enemies), which is why the term stuck. This article covers the season. See 09-events for the standalone Doomsday event.

---

TL;DR

Season 1 (officially "The Crimson Plague") is Last War: Survival's first competitive season — a 48-day virus-themed campaign preceded by a 2-week Pre-Season and followed by a ~4-5 week Celebration phase. The map, called the Land of Fog and Rain, is grouped into 8 warzones/servers competing for a central prize called the City of Apocalypse (a level-7 super-capitol) which unlocks on Day 28. All player power is gated behind a new stat — Virus Resistance — which is grown almost exclusively by upgrading the Virus Research Institute (max +10,000 across 30 levels). Without enough Virus Resistance, damage output can drop by 60-99% and incoming damage can be inflated by up to 900%, making the stat mandatory for city/stronghold captures and Doom Elite kills. Alliance ranking is decided by Alliance Influence Points accumulated from held cities (100k–1M each depending on level 1-6), culminating in the cross-warzone Infinite Octagon tournament in weeks 5-7 (see 13-meta-competitive for the tournament tier structure). Small alliances that cannot compete for city ranks may permanently switch to Builder Alliance mode in weeks 4-7 to trade cities-and-glory for stable construction rewards.

The term "Doomsday" that alliance chats use for Season 1 is a misnomer inherited from the "Doom Elite" / "Doom Walker" enemy family. Official name: The Crimson Plague. Known KR name: 크림슨 플레이그 (시즌 1).

Core mechanics

1. Season structure and duration

Season 1 runs on this cadence, in order:

  1. Pre-Season — 2 weeks. Preview mechanics, Season Boost bonus display, Season Preview (unlock story tiles by hitting daily goals), and a Pre-Season Capitol Conquest on Day 6 of Week 2. During pre-season Capitol Conquest, any alliance can join without needing adjacent land — this is intentionally more open than the mid-season Capitol rules.
  2. Active Season — 8 weekly cycles (~56 days; theriagames reports 48 days — a ~1-week disagreement possibly reflecting a patch or "active content" vs "wind-down" terminology drift; see also 06-season-2-frozen for the same S2 disagreement). All seasonal buildings, resources, virus mechanics and territory-capture rules are live.
  3. Celebration Phase — ~4-5 weeks. Black Market Event (28 days), Bingo Task (Week 1), Champion Duel tournament, and Black Market Cash weekly challenges (Weeks 2-4). Seasonal buildings and their power bonuses are removed at the end of Celebration; permanent rewards (medals, hero shards, exclusive weapon activations) stay.

The season repeats mission "weeks" internally: each mission has a start day and a mission goal that unlocks a delayed number of days later. The whole 8-week schedule is server-time synchronised across all 8 warzones in a group so that Warzone-vs-Warzone events line up.

2. The map — Land of Fog and Rain

The map is a virus-infested world where every city, stronghold, and strategic tile begins under the control of the Crimson Legion (the NPC faction). Alliances spawn at map edges in hives; the center of the map is the endgame prize (City of Apocalypse, level 7). Everything must be re-captured from the Legion.

Territory types:

  • Military Strongholds (level 1 only) — the entry-level territory. Guarded by a Corruptor (a new NPC mini-boss). Adjacency to at least one Stronghold you own is required to declare on Cities.
  • Cities, levels 1 through 6. Levels unlock on a schedule (see numbers table). Each city has a "durability" that scales with level and generates Alliance Influence Points while held.
  • City of Apocalypse (level 7, map centre) — the former capital of the world, now Legion-held. Unlocks on Day 28. Same rules as Capitol Capture but 8-hour contest.
  • Contaminated Land (검은땅 in KR). PvP zones where cross-warzone attack is permitted (on foreign warzones every day; on your own warzone only on Saturdays).
  • Alliance Outposts. Placed on foreign warzones during Warzone Expedition (Week 3+). Max 4 outposts per alliance, one per foreign warzone. Construction-protected on placement.
3. Capital City — City of Apocalypse

The Capitol from base-game (the alliance-controlled capital where ministries and hats are assigned) rolls forward into Season 1: the alliance holding the Capitol before Season 1 starts retains it and keeps all Ministry access for the first 28 days. After Day 28 the Capitol becomes contestable again under a modified ruleset that requires holding an adjacent level-6 city or a bordering stronghold.

At map centre sits the City of Apocalypse, a level-7 super-capitol. Rules for contesting it:

  • Unlocks Day 28
  • 3-day protection removal delay (Week 4 mission goal)
  • Contest window: 8 hours
  • Victory: first alliance to hold 4 cumulative hours wins, or highest cumulative hold-time at the buzzer
  • Winner gains access to bordering territory chains
  • Only alliances that hold level-6 cities may participate (adjacency requirement)
4. Strongholds, Cities and capture rules

Daily capture limits (own warzone, before Warzone Expedition):

  • Strongholds: 2/day, across all warzones combined
  • Cities: 2/day (on your home warzone in week 1-3)

Once Warzone Expedition opens (Week 3, Day 16):

  • Cities may be declared only on Thursdays and Saturdays ("declaration days") — this applies to both home warzone and foreign warzones alike
  • Rally-based attacks still consume the daily limit; unsuccessful attacks do not consume a capture slot

Maximum territory held:

  • Strongholds base cap: 3
  • +1 stronghold slot per level-1 through level-5 city captured
  • +2 stronghold slots per level-6 city captured
  • Cities: 4 max on home warzone + 4 across foreign warzones = 8 total

Example: an alliance holding 3× lvl-4 cities + 1× lvl-6 city can hold 3 + 3 + 2 = 8 strongholds.

Protection status:

  • Strongholds: 36 hours after successful capture
  • Cities: 7 days after successful capture — protection expires at 12:00 server time on the 6th day. Protection persists even if the city is abandoned mid-window.
  • Swap tactic (KR discovery): swap a city on Thursday → protection expires next Thursday; swap Saturday → next Saturday. Used for locking down protection windows the week before season end.

Adjacency and declaration:

  • Your alliance territory must be connected via owned Strongholds to the target City
  • Exception: the Capitol and City of Apocalypse can bridge to adjacent territories without a Stronghold chain
  • War declaration duration: 1 hour from posting

Casualties / virus damage:

  • Troops are infected during city sieges regardless of resistance
  • If your Virus Resistance is below the city's virus strength, damage is reduced 60% (mild gap) to 99% (critical gap)
  • Enemy damage to you scales up: +375% (mild gap) up to +900% (critical gap)
5. Virus Resistance — the master stat

Virus Resistance is the single most important stat in Season 1. Every other seasonal system — Profession, Genetic Recombination, territory captures — exists to build Virus Resistance faster.

  • Only source of Virus Resistance: Virus Research Institute (VRI), max level 30, total contribution ranging +100 to +500 per level, cap +10,000.
  • Season Pass adds +250 Virus Resistance (7-day buff, repeatable weekly).
  • Battle bonuses from Professions and Genetic Recombination add more resistance.

Damage penalty for insufficient resistance (published by cpt-hedge & ldshop.gg calculators): | Resistance gap | Damage dealt | Damage taken | |----------------|--------------|--------------| | Meets/exceeds required | Normal | Normal | | Small gap | −60% | +375% | | Critical gap | −99% | +900% |

Confirmed 2026-07-15. The −60% / −99% dmg-dealt and +375% / +900% dmg-taken numbers are now cross-sourced from a Tier B guide walking through a concrete example (Virus Resistance 350 vs 500 required = "damage drops by 60%" and "monster damage +375%"; larger critical gap escalates to "99% penalty" and "up to 900% incoming damage") [ldshop.gg/blog/last-war-survival/season-1-virus-guide.html via search excerpt, 2026-07-15]. Same damage curve is shared across Seasons 1–5 per lastwarhandbook.com/guides/season-1-virus-infection-guide (search excerpt, 2026-07-15) — the resistance values required change per season but the reduction math is a shared shared function. Previously KB-flagged as weakly-sourced (cpt-hedge 403); now upgraded to two Tier B independent confirmations.

For rally leaders attacking strongholds: only the leader's resistance is checked. If the leader is under-resistant, the entire rally's damage drops to zero and casualties are massive. This is why R5/R4 alliances usually front-load VRI upgrades on their rally leaders.

6. Virus / infection system

RA Virus Stacks are the infection meter: 0 to 100.

  • Thresholds at 1, 20, 40, 60, 80 stacks activate progressive debuffs (reduced damage, increased casualties, morale penalties).
  • 100 stacks: base explodes after a countdown, damaging and infecting other bases in the immediate blast radius.
  • Infection sources: attacking infected zombies above your resistance, attacking Corruptors (auto-infect regardless of resistance), attacking Doom Elite (giant scorpions), being attacked by other infected bases, weather.
  • Treatment: allied players use Immune Protein to cure you (airplane mechanic). Curing another player earns gene fragments.

8. Profession Hall (Careers)

A one-time-per-season choice between Engineer and War Leader.

  • Engineer: construction/research/production bonuses (economy-facing). Preferred by farm accounts and by big-hive R5s who want steady buildout.
  • War Leader: combat/rally bonuses. Preferred by main-account rally leaders.

Two skill columns:

  • Left (blue) — permanent skills carried across seasons
  • Right (green) — seasonal skills that reset at season end

Seasonal skills unlock at levels 1, 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 (9 tiers). Includes Field Veteran (raises daily Profession-XP-from-zombies cap from 75k → 150k) and the Viral Offense function (allows spreading virus offensively).

10. Seasonal resources

Three currencies (they don't survive the season):

  • Immune Protein — from Protein Farms → upgrades VRI + Military Bases + used to cure allies
  • Mutant Crystal — from zombie kills + events → upgrades Protein Farms + Military Bases
  • Gene Fragments (Serum) — 6 types (I–VI); dropped by Doom Elite, from curing allies, and from Serum Collector event → used in Genetic Recombination

Genetic Recombination event:

  • Duration 47 days (starts Day 3 of Week 1)
  • 6 fragment types, exchanged with allies to complete sets
  • Recombination pool: starts at 10, regenerates +1 every 2 hours, cap 30
  • Payout: Mutant Crystals + buffs
11. Warzone Expedition & cross-server war

Warzone grouping: 8 servers per Season 1 group, competing simultaneously.

Timeline:

  • Week 3, Day 16 — Warzone Expedition opens; alliances can place outposts on foreign warzones (max 4 per alliance, 1 per foreign warzone). Outposts open at Stronghold levels 2, 4, 6 or 8.
  • Week 4, Day 1 — Warzone Declaration mission starts; first Capitol Declaration of War happens end of Week 4.
  • Week 5-7 — Infinite Octagon, the flagship cross-server tournament (see below).

Kill rules by day of week:

  • Every day: attack anyone in contaminated land of foreign warzones
  • Saturdays: always PvP-open across all warzones (bases and squads attackable everywhere) with cross-warzone teleportation
  • Thursdays and Saturdays: City war declarations allowed (both home and foreign warzones once Expedition opens)
12. Infinite Octagon (S1 tournament, Weeks 5-7)

The Season 1 endgame. Officially documented on the Last War support site (First Fun Group).

  • 3 rounds spanning Weeks 5-7
  • 8 warzones per bracket — single-elimination, no consolation round for losing warzones [confirmed 2026-07-15 via firstfungroup.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/44659758784147-S1-Infinite-Octagon search excerpt (Tier A official — direct fetch 403); corroborated by theriagames.com Season 1 calendar via search 2026-07-15]. The "Octagon" name is not decorative — it literally refers to the 8-warzone bracket. Previously KB-flagged as "inferred from bracket size" — now upgraded to Tier A confirmation from the official First Fun support article.
  • Servers are grouped by warzone Influence Points (highest-ranked group is the "Golden Bracket")
  • Each round: two servers duel; winner is decided by Bonus Points accumulated over the round
  • Winning warzone earns bulk Warzone Influence Points
  • Alliance-side bonus (if alliance total Influence Points > 10,000,000,000 = 10 billion):
  • 1 round won: +250,000 alliance influence pts
  • 2 rounds won: +500,000
  • 3 rounds won: +1,500,000

14. Builder Alliance (opt-out mode, Weeks 4-7)

For alliances that realistically cannot compete for city rankings, Builder Alliance mode opens Week 4. Switching to Builder Alliance is irreversible for the rest of the season.

  • Trade: give up City Race + Influence ranking + top decoration (God of Judgment) + Season Merit Medals
  • Gain: stable donation-driven construction rewards (buildings you donate materials to → alliance-wide hero buffs + shard rewards)
  • Audience: small alliances, mid-tier alliances, casual/off-timezone alliances, migrant alliances
  • Choose window: Weeks 4-7 (can switch anytime in that range)
15. Landing patterns & hive placement (Season 1 practice)

Season 1 hive placement is the single highest-leverage pre-season decision by R5. Formal community consensus:

  1. Land near the alliance's target level-6 city — the endgame trophy for tier 1-3 rewards. Ideally within 1-2 stronghold-hops.
  2. Space bases tightly — Alliance Help reaches every base within a small radius; Zombie Siege reinforcements arrive in time; Desert Storm participation is available to the full roster; Warzone Duel defensive perimeter is cohesive.
  3. Avoid landing directly adjacent to the strongest hostile alliance — Season 1 daily-kill windows on Saturdays make hive-on-hive borders violent.
  4. Reserve interior slots for R5/R4 rally leaders — they should be flanked by farm accounts so incoming attackers hit farms first.
  5. Hive compliance: members leaving the hive without R5 approval get one warning then removal. Compliance is tracked like VS-day participation.

No published "canonical coordinate" list exists — the map is randomised per warzone group. Alliance R5s pre-plan hive shape (see the Alliance Hive Grid Manager tool the user has built) then anchor on the corresponding coordinates once the map is revealed.

16. Veteran / Prestige / Eternal War

Research finding: the terms "veteran mode," "prestige mode," and "eternal war" do not appear in any official Last War: Survival documentation for Season 1. They may be community-invented shorthand from other games (Last Shelter: Survival's "Doomsday" event has an "eternal war" mode; Last War is a different game).

  • Field Veteran exists but is a Profession skill (raises the daily Profession XP cap from zombies from 75k to 150k), not a game mode.
  • No Season 1 replay / prestige / veteran-tier progression was found in any tier-A or tier-B source. Once Season 1 ends and Celebration passes, the world transitions to Season 2 (Frozen / Polar Storm). Seasons then run 1 → 2 → 3 (Golden Kingdom) → 4 (Evernight Isle) → 5 (Wild West) → 6 (Lost Rainforest) chronologically per server.
  • Whether servers replay Season 1 after Season 6, or whether "eternal war" refers to a post-S6 endgame: unconfirmed — filed to open questions.

Some sources hint that once a warzone hits the top season, it repeats the current season on a rolling basis, but this was not verifiable from primary sources.

16.8 Corruptor / Doom Elite key facts
  • Corruptor guards each Military Stronghold. Attacking always infects you regardless of resistance. Deals massive damage if your VR is under its virus level.
  • Doom Elite (giant infected scorpions) roam contaminated land — big Genetic Fragment drops. Rally required; leader's VR is checked.
  • Doom Walker — the boss from the standalone Doomsday event (not S1-specific), 4h spawns on event days, first-kill reward claimable up to 3× per event.
16.8b Pumpkin Cauldron base skin (S1-tied premium cosmetic)

Clarification 2026-07-15. The KB previously flagged Pumpkin Cauldron as a possible pre-season event of unknown mechanics (open-question #5). It is now confirmed to be a legendary base skin, not a mechanic-event. Verified via the official Last War: Survival X/Twitter beginner's-guide post on Season Boost base skins [x.com/lastwarsurvival status 1922480680744976704, 2025-05-14, Tier A official — "Season 1 — Pumpkin Cauldron: Increases virus stack limit!"] and the Last War wiki entry [lastwar.wiki/customizations/pumpkin-cauldron, Tier B, 2026-07-15 search excerpt].

  • Type: Legendary base skin (Season-1-themed Halloween cosmetic)
  • Season Boost effect: Increases virus stack limit — permanent +50 virus stack limit (so your base can absorb 150 stacks instead of the base 100 before the explosion countdown triggers)
  • Unlocks: the Virus Surge active base skill
  • Social mechanic: any commander who "likes" your base earns a Pumpkin Gift (5 speedups)
  • Price: premium bundle; historically reached up to ~$400 USD (Halloween 2025 promotion)

Interaction: the +50 stack ceiling essentially raises the point at which infection-based self-destruct becomes a risk, which is enormously valuable during multi-day Contaminated Land pushes where accumulating stacks is unavoidable. See §6 (virus/infection system) for baseline 100-stack ceiling behavior — Pumpkin Cauldron users effectively play by a 150-stack ceiling on all thresholds through the season.

16.11 Season 1 formation meta (community consensus, 2025-2026)

The KB previously listed a formation-meta gap (open-question #8). Now backed by cross-source community consensus [packsify.com/blogs/best-squad-lineup-in-last-war-survival-game, 2026 update, Tier C; allclash.com/best-team-formations-lineups-in-last-war-survival-game, 2025 update, Tier C; ldshop.gg/blog/last-war-survival/season-1-hero-recommendations.html, Tier B; all via search 2026-07-15].

Master rule — RPS triangle Aircraft > Tank > Missile > Aircraft per 01-core-gameplay. Full 5-hero same-type squad grants +20% squad-wide stat bonus, which is why mono-type formations dominate over mixed.

Early-game S1 meta — Tank squad (~Week 1 through Day ~60-70):

  • Murphy + Williams + Kimberly + Stetmann + Marshall
  • Rationale: Kimberly is the highest-DPS early-game hero (S1 Rocket Shadow debut Wk1 D4 makes her even stronger from day 4). Murphy + Williams absorb hits so Kimberly + Stetmann DPS survives. Marshall adds attack-speed buffs.
  • Fits S1's early-game because virus resistance is still low across the server → tank frontline soaks Corruptor damage during Stronghold captures.

Mid-to-late-game S1 meta — Aircraft squad (~Day 70+):

  • Lucius + Carlie + DVA + Morrison + Schuyler
  • Rationale: as the server matures and most alliances default to tanks (because tanks dominate early PvE too), the RPS counter is aircraft. Full-aircraft squad picks up +20% mono-bonus AND the +20% RPS advantage vs tanks = ~44% multiplicative damage edge.
  • DVA is called out as S-tier "fits every meta." Tesla is the equivalent for the Missile line.

Counter-pick — Missile squad (situational, ~Day 60+ if server is aircraft-dominated):

  • Anchored by Tesla; specific 5-hero list varies by roster availability.
  • Only run this if scouting confirms your warzone's top alliances have gone all-aircraft; otherwise the meta stays tank/aircraft.

S1-specific twist not present in the general formation guides: rally leaders in S1 must also meet the Stronghold/City's Virus Resistance requirement or the rally deals ~0 damage (see §5). So the "best formation" for the leader isn't purely DPS-optimal — it's DPS-optimal given the leader has stacked enough VRI to clear the resistance gate. Farm accounts often run the "correct" formation for pure PvE, while rally leaders bias toward whichever hero-comp lets them survive the resistance-gap damage penalty.
