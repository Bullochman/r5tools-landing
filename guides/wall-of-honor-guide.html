Shard sources (per Theria Games, LDShop, Cpt Hedge):

  • Tavern — daily free recruits (mostly SR fodder, occasional SSR).
  • Elite Recruitment — SSR-guaranteed with SSR + UR shard mix.
  • Advanced (Legendary) Recruitment — UR-focused pool with ~1% UR rate, ~4% SSR rate.
  • Hero Wish — spend 200 wish tickets → 10 shards of a specific named hero. Primary way to farm a target UR without RNG.
  • Campaign farming — targeted SSR shards drop from specific stages. Mason (S1 promotion candidate) drops at Normal Stage 4-3 (1–2 per clear); Violet (S2) drops at Normal Stage 8-5 (1–3 per clear).
  • Trade & Plunder — attacking trucks yields ~2 shards each.
  • Honor Store, Campaign Store, Alliance Store — periodic hero shard rotations.
  • Battle Pass — every season includes a Growth / Hero pass with named UR shards on tracks.
  • Radar & event stores — Arms Race, Alliance Duels, Capital War all pay out shard bundles as milestones.
  • Black Market (post-season) — currency accumulated over the season is spent here for shard choice boxes; the primary F2P long-tail source.

Requirements:

  • Hero at 5★ SSR (975 SSR shards invested).
  • Season-specific UR Hero Badge earned from that season's goals.
  • Recommended (not mandatory): Wall of Honor level ~160 on that hero.

Wall of Honor (permanent hero-account passive)

The Wall of Honor is the account-level "extra shards keep helping" system. Unlock requires:

  • Hero at 5★.
  • Corresponding troop building (Tank Center / Aircraft Center / Missile Center) at Level 20.

Every extra shard beyond 5★ raises the hero's Wall entry level. Each Wall entry gives a specific stat bonus that applies to your account permanently regardless of whether that hero is fielded. Every 50 levels the per-tier bonus increases; there is no level cap on Wall of Honor entries.

Per Lootbar's Wall of Honor guide, published bonus types per hero:

  • Mason: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)
  • Cage: +0.50% Defense
  • Lucius: +0.50% Defense
  • Carlie: +0.50% Health
  • Tesla: +0.25% Attack
  • DVA: +0.50% Defense
  • Morrison: +0.50% Attack
  • Schuyler: +0.50% Attack
  • Violet: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)
  • Maxwell: +0.25% Attack
  • Fiona: +0.50% Attack
  • Swift: +0.50% Attack
  • Kimberly: +0.50% Attack [Last War Vault Wall of Honor Guide, 2026-07-15; corroborated by lastwar.wiki Wall of Honor page, 2026-07-15 — corrects a prior KB entry that read "+0.50% Defense", which was a KB transcription error; Kimberly is the flagship AoE-DPS Tank and grants the strongest tank Attack passive in the Wall of Honor]
  • Scarlett: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)

UR heroes typically give the +0.50% class of bonuses; lower-rarity gives +0.25% class bonuses. The Wall entry compounds every 50 levels — 5-star + long-tail shard investment can push individual heroes to 300+ Wall level over a year of play. This is the primary long-term reason to keep dumping SSR shards into a fully-starred hero.

2026-07-15 cross-check of Tank-hero bonuses — Last War Vault's Wall of Honor guide (Tier B) publishes the full Tank block as: Kimberly / Mason (UR) → +0.50% Attack; Marshall / Stetmann → +0.50% Defense; Murphy / Williams / Violet (UR) → +0.50% Health; Loki / Gump → +1.00% Troop Load. lastwar.wiki confirms Kimberly = Attack. This resolves the "Kimberly = Defense" question flagged in the KB's Contradictions section.

Wall of Honor ROI (long-term F2P investment)

The Wall of Honor is described across multiple 2026 sources as the single best long-term progression system in the game for F2P and low-spend accounts. The value proposition:

  • No level cap — every extra shard past 5★ compounds indefinitely.
  • Account-wide passive — bonuses apply whether the hero is fielded or benched.
  • Refund on promotion — SSR → UR promotion refunds shards spent on Wall entries for that hero, so investing Wall levels into Mason/Violet/etc. is zero-risk if you plan to promote them [lootbar.com wall-of-honor guide, 2026].
  • Every 50 Wall levels the per-tier bonus increases by +0.25% to +1.0% — meaning a hero at Wall Lv.100 has a materially stronger bonus than at Lv.50, and Lv.250 stronger still [lootbar.com, 2026; heaven-guardian.com wall-of-honor guide, 2026].

Optimal F2P Wall strategy (Lootbar / Handbook consensus):

  1. Accumulate shards, don't dump them. Save excess shards for Versus Day (VS Day) events — the shard-dump earns free VS Day event points on top of the Wall progression.
  2. Prioritize Attack bonuses over Defense/HP bonuses for damage-dealer heroes on your rally comp — Attack compounds harder with existing gear/tech/formation multipliers.
  3. Promote to UR when eligible — this refunds the SSR shard investment, letting you reallocate to the next Wall target.
  4. Focus on 3–5 priority heroes, not all 30+. Community consensus: "Identify your three to five priority Heroes before spending any shards. Do not invest in heroes outside this shortlist" [lastwarhandbook.com hero-recruitment-tickets, 2026].

Exact shard costs per Wall level are not published in any English guide reviewed — Lootbar, Handbook, LastWarVault all describe the +50-level breakpoint pattern without publishing per-level shard curves. This is flagged in Open Questions.
