Last War: Survival runs a strict three-way rock-paper-scissors combat system: Aircraft beats Tank, Tank beats Missile, Missile beats Aircraft, with each counter granting +20% damage dealt and −20% damage taken. Troops come in the same three "vehicle" classes (Tank / Aircraft / Missile Vehicle — no infantry/lancer/marksman split, that's Whiteout Survival), organized in tiers T1–T11 gated by Barracks level (T10 at Barracks 30, T11 at Barracks 35 plus Armament Institute research). Each troop has four battle stats — Attack, Defense, Health, Lethality — and is deployed as part of a 5-hero squad (2 front / 3 back) that gains stacking mono-type formation bonuses up to +20% Attack/Defense/HP for a full-type stack. Combat losses are triaged into three buckets: lightly wounded (auto-heal), wounded (Hospital), fainted / severely wounded (Emergency Center at fainted-unit ratio 21%→40%); anything past both caps is permanently killed. Rallies combine up to 5 alliance joiners under the leader's stats — the leader's research, gear, hero levels, and drone bonuses apply to every joiner's troops, so leader selection is the single largest lever in alliance combat (see 08-alliance-systems for rally-coordination context).

T11 — Armament Institute and specializations

T11 troops (branded "Level 11 Unit" in-game) shipped in the Age of Oil season. To unlock (cpt-hedge t11 guide; ldshop t11 guide; earnne support guide; official firstfun zendesk):

  1. Season 4 must be completed (off-season phase)
  2. HQ ≥ 27 to unlock the Armament Institute (single-level, non-upgradable building)
  3. Barracks ≥ 35 to actually train T11
  4. All four Armament Institute researches to 100%, completed in strict order:
  5. Helmet — required first
  6. Body Armor (Armor)
  7. Protective Gear (Boots in some translations)
  8. Weapon

Every level takes Armament Materials; every 5th level of the first three branches (and every 2nd–3rd level of Weapon) additionally requires an Armament Core. Final unlock consumes 120,000 Armament Materials + 200 Armament Cores + 1.2M Oil.

Timeline expectations (earnne):

  • Free-to-play: 4–5 months
  • Light spenders: 2–3 months
  • Heavy spenders: 1–2 months

T11 has two specializations you can swap between every 72 hours:

SpecializationPassiveBest for
Armored TrooperBattle Protection — severe wound rate reduced by 5%Defense, war zones, situations where hospital + emergency center overflow
Assault RaiderIron Will — penalty for not being at full march capacity reduced by 10%Offense, rallies you rejoin without refilling, running less-than-full marches

Base stats are identical between the two — only the passive differs. Stat gains vs. T10: roughly 5–10% across Attack, Defense, HP, Squad Load, Morale, and Power (earnne).

Armament star bonuses (require all four branches at the same star level to activate):

  • ★1: Both types ignore part of enemy "Reduce Damage Taken" effects
  • ★2: Counterattack / restoration effects
  • ★3: Increased Lethality rate when attacking
  • ★4: Armored Trooper further reduces severe wound rate; Assault Raider further reduces the not-full-capacity penalty

Note: First Fun's official developer guidance (firstfungroup zendesk) explicitly says that in World Map PvP where healing cycles fast, the 5% severe wound reduction is not a large practical advantage — implying Assault Raider is the default for most players unless running a pure defense meta.

T11 stat delta over T10 — post-launch cross-check (2026-07-15). Both T11 specializations share identical base stats — the specialization only changes the passive proc (Battle Protection vs Iron Will), not the underlying Attack/Defense/HP/Lethality numbers. Post-launch stat delta over maxed T10 has two competing published estimates: (a) an early Lootbar preview article speculated +18–22% Attack, +12% HP and was explicitly flagged as pre-release rumor at the time; (b) post-launch guide-aggregator sources (per Last War Handbook troop-tier comparison, search excerpt 2026-07-15) now cite the effective post-launch delta at closer to +5–10% Attack/HP over T10. Reconciliation: the Lootbar preview was likely including Armament star bonuses (★1–★4) in the "T11 stats" number, while the post-launch aggregator numbers are base-T11-only. Neither source publishes a per-stat table (Attack / Defense / HP / Lethality) with exact integers — the definitive numbers still require an in-game pull. See Open Questions.
