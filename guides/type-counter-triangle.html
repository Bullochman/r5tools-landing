Heroes in Last War: Survival are the single largest lever on squad power. There are three combat types — Tank (탱크), Aircraft (전투기), and Missile Vehicle (미사일) — in a rock-paper-scissors: Tank > Missile, Missile > Aircraft, Aircraft > Tank, at roughly ±20% damage (see 04-squads-combat for the damage-formula math). Heroes exist at four rarities — R, SR, SSR, UR — with SSR→UR promotion available for one designated hero per season (Mason S1, Violet S2, Scarlett S3, Sarah S4, Venom S5, Braz S6). Every hero has four skills (Auto Attack, Tactical/Active, Passive, Expertise), reaches 5★ with 975 shards, and unlocks progression tiers at 1★/2★/3★/5★. Season 6 (Shadow/Lost Rainforest, launched 2026-04-13) introduced Hero Awakening, which replaces the "Exclusive Weapon" system for new UR seasonal releases and adds a 4th-skill overlay to legacy heroes (Kimberly Week 1, DVA Week 3, Tesla Week 5). The current meta is Tank-dominant, built around Kimberly (UR AoE carry) with Williams/Murphy as anchors and Marshall as the only UR support in the game.

  • R (Rare, common) — starter units. Barely used past the tutorial. Nomenclature varies; some sources call them "Common."
  • SR (Super Rare, blue) — four heroes total, all damage dealers: Ambolt, Gump, Kane, Loki. Loki is the strongest of the group thanks to a shotgun burst kit; Ambolt is usually the first Aircraft hero every account touches. Rarely competitive past HQ 20 but useful for filler slots, event side-teams, and early campaign clears. Levelable with Rare Hero Universal Shards, which are the cheapest and most plentiful shard type.
  • SSR (Epic, purple) — the mid-tier bulk of the roster (13+ heroes). Includes Mason, Violet, Scarlett, Sarah, Venom, Braz, Cage, Elsa, Maxwell, Monica, Richard, Farhad, Kane (SR), and others. Six of these are "SSR→UR promotion" candidates (see below). A 5★ SSR generally out-performs a 3★ UR, so SSRs are the F2P bridge until UR heroes cross 4★.
  • UR (Legendary, orange/red) — the endgame roster. 15+ heroes as of Season 6, one added per season on average. Divided across Tank, Aircraft, and Missile. Murphy is the only free-to-play UR who begins in every account's inventory; every other UR requires either shards from Legendary Recruitment, seasonal battle passes, or SSR→UR promotion.

Nomenclature check: the community calls the three types Tank / Aircraft / Missile. Some early guides use "Missile Vehicle" (the full in-game label) and rarely "Lancer" — the latter is a translation artifact from a different game and is not standard LWS terminology. Korean uses 탱크 (tank), 전투기 (fighter/aircraft), 미사일 (missile); there is no equivalent to "Lancer" in the Korean UI.

  1. Auto Attack (top-left) — fires automatically each time the attack cooldown resolves. Physical or Energy damage class depending on hero. Priority to upgrade only for pure DPS carries; tank Auto Attacks are decorative and should be skipped on the medal queue.
  2. Tactical (Active) skill (top-right) — the hero's signature move. Fires on a cooldown after battle start (usually 10–20s), then repeats. This is where the biggest single-skill power spikes live. First priority on medals for damage dealers.
  3. Passive (bottom-left) — always-on aura or self-buff. Priority for tanks (survivability) and supports (team buffs).
  4. Expertise (bottom-right) — unlocks at 4★. For nearly every UR hero this is "Super Sensing," a flat +20% self HP/ATK/DEF plus +10% skill cooldown speed. Season 6 Awakening replaces the Expertise skill with a hero-specific 4th skill that inherits the +20/+10 bonuses.

There is no separate "expedition skill" system in LWS despite that wording appearing in tier-list articles. The four skills above apply in both the main map (Doomsday / Frozen / Rainforest zombie waves), Alliance Duel, Capital War, Season War, Global Expedition, and PvE campaigns. What differs by mode is the squad-level buff overlays (formation type bonus, tech-center research, alliance emblems, tactical drone) — those are separate from the hero's own skills.

Every hero levels through five stars using hero shards. Costs are identical across rarities in shard count; only the type of shard differs.

Squad formation and type bonus

Squads hold 5 heroes, arranged 2 front-row / 3 back-row. Formation rules:

  • Same-type bonus (모노 스쿼드) — a squad where all 5 heroes share a type gets up to ~20% to primary stats per the KR BlueStacks guide; the Packsify guide phrases this as "up to 44% effective power" once tech and gear overlays are counted. The specific stat multiplier at max stack is disputed across sources but the mechanic is uncontroversial: mono-type squads dominate.
  • Type counter — Tank deals +20% damage to Missile and takes –20% from Missile; Missile similarly counters Aircraft; Aircraft similarly counters Tank. Confirmed in both EN (Pocket Gamer, Packsify) and KR (BlueStacks KR, LDPlayer KR) sources. This is the rock-paper-scissors backbone of all PvP formation decisions.
  • Hybrid "4+1" Marshall setup — Marshall is the only UR support in the game, and pairing four same-type heroes with Marshall retains ~35% of the pure-type formation bonus while gaining Marshall's global ATK amplification. Wardawgg and Packsify call this "the most efficient setup for 90% of players."

Slot positioning matters: front row (slots 1–2) takes physical damage first and needs tanks; back row (slots 3–5) should hold DPS and support. Williams and Murphy are the two mandatory front-row tanks in nearly every meta comp. Kimberly, DVA, Tesla, and Marshall sit in the back row.
