Last War: Survival runs on an 8-week seasonal treadmill: every server rotates through a fresh themed map with new resources, buildings, PvP events, and hero/weapon rollouts. Since Season 2 (Polar Storm) ended, First Fun has shipped four full seasons — S3 Golden Kingdom (Egyptian desert / Spice Wars / Curse Resistance), S4 Evernight Isle (Sakura island / Lighthouse / Blood Night), S5 Wild West (Golden Wasteland / Banks / Trade Train / merged 8-warzone map), and S6 Shadow Rainforest (faction warfare / permanent city destruction / Hero Awakening). Season 7 is confirmed by community creators as launching approximately August 2026 but no official theme/mechanic details have leaked publicly at the time of this research (2026-07-14). Each season introduces a new seasonal resource loop, a new PvP conflict pattern, and a designated hero for UR ascension or Awakening — and the trend from S5→S6 is toward fewer, longer war windows and permanent, high-stakes territorial outcomes rather than continuous grind (documented in 13-meta-competitive).

The seasonal cadence — what's fixed across every season

Since Season 3, every LWS season has followed the same structural template, with only theme and specific mechanics varying:

  • 2-week pre-season (introduced in S4, retained through S6): countdown, faction/warzone grouping, rule preview, reward preview, Season Boost, capitol conquest without adjacency, Alliance Safe Time configuration.
  • 7-day Season Preview — daily challenges unlock the season's mechanics one at a time before main season begins.
  • 8-week main season — the actual war phase, divided into weekly milestones (city unlocks, faction contests, war windows).
  • Celebration / off-season week — final rewards, T-troop unlocks (T11 introduced in S4), migration prep to next season.
  • Server migration — at season end, players' accounts, HQ level, heroes, and tech carry forward; the map and all seasonal buildings/resources reset. Servers merge across warzones as populations decay (08-alliance-systems cross-ref).

The 8-week main season subdivides consistently: Weeks 1–3 are build-out (buildings unlocked progressively, seasonal resource stockpiling), Weeks 4–6 are the main PvP conflict (Spice Wars / Copper Wars / City Clash / Faction Duel prelims), and Weeks 7–8 are the season-defining endgame (Capitol War, Ancestral Temple, Faction Duel final).

Length has crept upward: S3 was documented as ~8 weeks; S4 as "roughly 8–10 weeks" including celebration; total cycle including pre-season, main, and post-season is ~14 weeks per KR namu-wiki interpretation. Sources disagree on whether S4/S5 celebration weeks count as "the season" or as inter-season buffer.

Season 3 — Golden Kingdom (Egyptian desert)

Launch: ~6–7 weeks after Season 2 concludes on a given server (rolling per-server launch, not a global date). Pre-season 14-day countdown appears below the VS duel button. Duration: 8 weeks. Theme: Noah has crowned himself ruler of the "Golden Kingdom" of ancient Egypt, unearthed the Monument of the Dead, and unleashed a mummy army and sandworms.

Map: 13×13 grid. Three building types populate the desert:

  • Cities (Levels 1–6, produce 100–600 Spice/hour)
  • Digging Strongholds (produce Mithril)
  • Trade Posts (individually captured; Levels 1–5 unlock progressively weeks 2–3)

New seasonal resources: | Resource | Purpose | Source | |---|---|---| | Spice | End-of-season ranking currency | Alliance Center production, Spice Wars victories, city control | | Mithril | Upgrades Blessing Fountains and Protector's Field | Doom Walker first blood, Desert Ruins radar missions, Mithril Plant | | Sacred Water | Upgrades Curse Research Lab | Blessing Fountains, first-time zombie kills | | Sandworm Scales | Desert Bazaar exchange currency (Protector Horns, Trap Landmine refills) | Sandworm Crisis event participation |

New seasonal buildings: | Building | Max Level | Function | |---|---|---| | Curse Research Lab | 30 | Increases Curse Resistance; L30 unlocks Scarlett UR ascension | | Blessing Fountain (5 variants) | 30 | Produces Sacred Water; unlocked progressively | | Altar | 30 | Buffs Desert Protector stats +0.5% per level | | Protector's Field | — | Houses Desert Protectors (one-time-use elite units) | | Alliance Center | — | Produces Spice, provides in-range buffs, prevents Sandworm knockback |

Alliance Center secondary building unlocks:

  • Level 5: Mithril Plant (34,560/hour)
  • Level 10: Exploratory Well (600 Oil/hour)
  • Level 15: Sacred Water Hut (8,640/hour)
  • Level 20: Obelisk (6 Protector Horns/hour)

Desert Protectors — the signature S3 combat mechanic. Disposable elite units converted from battlefield casualties (10% of losses recovered via Recon Plane assist). Progressive squad buffs based on stationed count:

  • 500+ units: Power of Adaptation — +250 Curse Resistance, +25% ATK/DEF/HP
  • 750+ units: Death Shockwave — damages enemy units when squad defeated
  • 1000+ units: Blue Flame Spell — 1-hour debuff on defeated enemy base
  • 1250+ units: Protector Rally — 3× march speed bonus (Protector squads only)

Each hero carries 1/10 of stationed protector count. All participating Protectors vanish after combat — this is a burnable resource, not a persistent army.

Curse Resistance — new stat that gates PvE content. If your Curse Resistance is below a monster's requirement, your damage drops sharply (up to 99.9% reduction). Rally leader's resistance applies to all participants unless individual members have higher levels. This is why the Curse Research Lab is the single most important S3 building to keep upgraded.

Curse Resistance formula precision (open question status 2026-07-16): Multiple sources confirm the qualitative shape — "damage drops to as much as 99.9%" when CR is under monster requirement — but the exact CR-shortfall → damage-multiplier table remains gated behind Cpt Hedgehog's paywalled Season 3 pages and the Last War Vault Curse Resistance guide's supporter-only content [lastwarvault.com/guides/season-3/curse-resistance-guide/ WebFetch 2026-07-16 confirmed paywall]. What the KB does know from published summaries: (a) the penalty is a smooth-ish curve, not a hard cliff — small CR shortfalls yield small damage cuts, large shortfalls yield the 99.9% wipe; (b) the rally leader's CR overrides participant CR only if the leader's is higher; (c) sandworm content is exempt from CR entirely. Downstream Season-3 tools should model CR as a rally-leader input with a "damage effectiveness" output of 100% if CR ≥ requirement, else scaled down toward 0.1% — the exact interpolation curve should be labeled TBD, from-in-game until a screenshot pull captures the S3 monster-CR-requirement table.

Sandworm Crisis event (14 days): Combat between alliances generates "shock waves"; accumulated shock waves spawn:

  • Large Sandworms — knock bases outside Alliance Center range to distant map locations (forced teleport)
  • Giant Sandworms — map-wide hazards, trapped via the Trap Landmine profession skill

Spice Wars (Weeks 4–8): Two factions battle twice weekly across stages — Waiting → Declaration → Invitation → Preparation → War → End. Attackers can steal up to 30% of enemy Spice by destroying secondary buildings (5% each) and the Alliance Center (10%).

Warlord Missile in Season 3 — larger blast than Season 2 (added 2026-07-16). The Warlord Missile mechanic ported from S2 fires a 35×35 tile Contaminated Land blast in S3 (vs S2's 25×25). Same 3-min prep window, same public-warlord-base broadcast during prep, same 72-hour cooldown on skill after successful use or intercept-cancel. The wider blast means the S2 "park 5 tiles from the Furnace" rule of thumb undershoots in S3 — plan Season-3 defense with a 10-tile clearance ring around any missile-targetable building. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 3 Spice Wars & Faction Duel search-excerpt 2026-07-16; lastwargame.online Season 3 mechanics search-excerpt 2026-07-16 — both Tier B; direct fetches 403]

Faction Duel (Week 7): Days 1–6 = Invasion Right Contest; Day 7 = Capitol War (controlling Capitol yields +10M Spice; each Cannon controlled = +5M Spice).

Oasis Greenification event: Staged expansion of desertified land. Weeks 1–4 restrict greenification to city perimeters; weeks 5–7 allow free expansion once regions cross 80% thresholds, unlocking level progression toward capitol control.

Alliance capture limits: 2 city war declarations/day, max 8 captured cities; 2 digging stronghold captures/day, max 8 captured.

Professions: Cap extended from 60 → 100. Diplomat unavailable. New seasonal skills: Sandworm Traps, Summon Mummies, Nature's Touch, Trap Landmines.

Heroes / weapons: Scarlett upgradeable to UR (week 3). Exclusive weapons unlock: Marshall (Week 1), Schuyler (Week 3), McGregor (Week 6) via dedicated battle passes.

Radar Missions — Desert Ruins: 7-level progression with 2.016% odds of reaching final stage. Yields Merit Medals, resources, escape mini-games. Alliance Compasses unlock at Level 7.
