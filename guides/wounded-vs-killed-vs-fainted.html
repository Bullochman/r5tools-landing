Wounded vs killed vs fainted — LWS troop-loss rules

The LWS troop-loss cascade — wounded goes to Hospital, overflow goes to ICU/Emergency Center at HQ 22, and killed is only the final overflow.

Last War: Survival runs a strict three-way rock-paper-scissors combat system: Aircraft beats Tank, Tank beats Missile, Missile beats Aircraft, with each counter granting +20% damage dealt and −20% damage taken. Troops come in the same three "vehicle" classes (Tank / Aircraft / Missile Vehicle — no infantry/lancer/marksman split, that's Whiteout Survival), organized in tiers T1–T11 gated by Barracks level (T10 at Barracks 30, T11 at Barracks 35 plus Armament Institute research). Each troop has four battle stats — Attack, Defense, Health, Lethality — and is deployed as part of a 5-hero squad (2 front / 3 back) that gains stacking mono-type formation bonuses up to +20% Attack/Defense/HP for a full-type stack. Combat losses are triaged into three buckets: lightly wounded (auto-heal), wounded (Hospital), fainted / severely wounded (Emergency Center at fainted-unit ratio 21%→40%); anything past both caps is permanently killed. Rallies combine up to 5 alliance joiners under the leader's stats — the leader's research, gear, hero levels, and drone bonuses apply to every joiner's troops, so leader selection is the single largest lever in alliance combat (see 08-alliance-systems for rally-coordination context).

Why it works. First wave clears garrison. Follow-up waves land while defender's troops are marching home or sitting wounded — no re-garrisoning happens in the seconds between waves, so waves 2–N attack an empty or under-defended base and do durability damage to structures. "Even the strongest alliance struggles to stay afloat under the sheer volume."

Wounded → Fainted → Killed math (explicit)

Beyond the qualitative cascade above, the explicit rule per-battle (Last War Handbook — Healing System Master Guide search snippet 2026-07-15; Last War Handbook — Troop Combat Math search 2026-07-15):

casualties = <side loses N troops this battle>

wounded  = min(casualties, hospital_free_capacity)
overflow = casualties - wounded

fainted  = floor(overflow × fainted_unit_ratio)
                         (Emergency Center L1 = 0.21, L20+ = 0.40)
fainted  = min(fainted, emergency_center_free_capacity)

killed   = casualties - wounded - fainted    (permanent, unrecoverable)

Worked example. Emergency Center L20 (0.40 ratio). Hospital has 500 free slots. Emergency Center has 1,000 free slots. Battle results in 3,000 casualties:

  • wounded = min(3000, 500) = 500 → Hospital
  • overflow = 3000 − 500 = 2500
  • fainted = min(floor(2500 × 0.40), 1000) = min(1000, 1000) = 1000 → Emergency Center
  • killed = 3000 − 500 − 1000 = 1500 → permanent loss

Critical: Hospital and Emergency Center caps are independent. Even if Hospital still had free slots, once Emergency Center overflows the excess is killed — not backfilled to Hospital. Corroborated by namu.wiki KR: "병원자리가 남아도 응급환자수가 초과되면 전사 판정됩니다" — "even if there are Hospital slots free, if the Emergency Center is over cap, [the excess] is judged killed in action."

Ratio economics — the fainted-unit-ratio jump from L1 (0.21) to L20 (0.40) is roughly 90% more rescued troops per overflowing battle. In a saturated defensive stand this is the single largest permanent-death-mitigation lever after the actual Hospital/EC capacity itself. Combined with T11 Armored Trooper's −5% severe-wound rate, the same 3,000-casualty battle above drops permadeath count by another ~150 troops on top of the ratio effect.

Healing cost ratio. Healing wounded/fainted troops costs ~50% of their training cost (Handbook healing summary) — one source (search excerpt via lastwargame.online, 2026-07-15) puts the effective savings even higher: "Healing is always 60–70% cheaper than retraining" once you account for the resource + time cost of full re-training vs. the flat heal price. Either way, maximizing wounded/fainted recovery is a bigger economic win than minimizing casualties in an unavoidable engagement.

Wounded → Fainted → Killed cascade

The single most important defensive system after combat resolution. When a battle ends, every casualty rolls through this cascade (lastwar.wiki hospital; lastwar.wiki emergency-center; Handbook healing guide summary; theriagames emergency center; namu.wiki 라스트워 서바이벌):

  1. Wounded troops go to the Hospital, up to its capacity (Hospital L1 = 650; L35 during Age of Oil = 2,100 with room via multiple hospitals to combine). While in Hospital they aren't in your active army; they cost resources + time to heal.
  1. Overflow past Hospital cap would normally be dead — but a Fainted Unit Ratio rescues a percentage of that overflow into the Emergency Center (ICU). The ratio starts at 21% at Emergency Center Level 1 and increases +1% per level, capping at 40% at Level 20+. The Emergency Center has its own capacity limit (starting 450 troops L1, up to 2,100 troops L35).
  1. Overflow past both Hospital + Emergency Center capacity = permanently killed. These troops are gone forever, not merely wounded.

Additional Emergency Center specifics:

  • Requires HQ 22 to construct
  • Rescue operations have a 2-day cooldown between heals (with slight variations at higher levels)
  • Hospital and Emergency Center capacities are independent — full Emergency Center = death even if Hospital still has slots (per namu.wiki KR source: "병원자리가 남아도 응급환자수가 초과되면 전사 판정됩니다")

T11 Armored Trooper's 5% severe-wound reduction modifies the initial wounded distribution — 5% fewer of your casualties are classified as severe (which then land in Emergency Center), meaning more of them are merely wounded (Hospital). The dev-official position is this is a minor advantage in fast-healing scenarios and matters most in overwhelming defensive stands where you're saturating both buildings.
