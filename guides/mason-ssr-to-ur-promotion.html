Mason SSR→UR promotion — is it worth it?

The Mason promotion math — 975 SSR + 1,600 UR shards, medal refund, farm sources at Normal 4-3, and why he's the only universally-endorsed promotion.

Heroes in Last War: Survival are the single largest lever on squad power. There are three combat types — Tank (탱크), Aircraft (전투기), and Missile Vehicle (미사일) — in a rock-paper-scissors: Tank > Missile, Missile > Aircraft, Aircraft > Tank, at roughly ±20% damage (see 04-squads-combat for the damage-formula math). Heroes exist at four rarities — R, SR, SSR, UR — with SSR→UR promotion available for one designated hero per season (Mason S1, Violet S2, Scarlett S3, Sarah S4, Venom S5, Braz S6). Every hero has four skills (Auto Attack, Tactical/Active, Passive, Expertise), reaches 5★ with 975 shards, and unlocks progression tiers at 1★/2★/3★/5★. Season 6 (Shadow/Lost Rainforest, launched 2026-04-13) introduced Hero Awakening, which replaces the "Exclusive Weapon" system for new UR seasonal releases and adds a 4th-skill overlay to legacy heroes (Kimberly Week 1, DVA Week 3, Tesla Week 5). The current meta is Tank-dominant, built around Kimberly (UR AoE carry) with Williams/Murphy as anchors and Marshall as the only UR support in the game.

  • R (Rare, common) — starter units. Barely used past the tutorial. Nomenclature varies; some sources call them "Common."
  • SR (Super Rare, blue) — four heroes total, all damage dealers: Ambolt, Gump, Kane, Loki. Loki is the strongest of the group thanks to a shotgun burst kit; Ambolt is usually the first Aircraft hero every account touches. Rarely competitive past HQ 20 but useful for filler slots, event side-teams, and early campaign clears. Levelable with Rare Hero Universal Shards, which are the cheapest and most plentiful shard type.
  • SSR (Epic, purple) — the mid-tier bulk of the roster (13+ heroes). Includes Mason, Violet, Scarlett, Sarah, Venom, Braz, Cage, Elsa, Maxwell, Monica, Richard, Farhad, Kane (SR), and others. Six of these are "SSR→UR promotion" candidates (see below). A 5★ SSR generally out-performs a 3★ UR, so SSRs are the F2P bridge until UR heroes cross 4★.
  • UR (Legendary, orange/red) — the endgame roster. 15+ heroes as of Season 6, one added per season on average. Divided across Tank, Aircraft, and Missile. Murphy is the only free-to-play UR who begins in every account's inventory; every other UR requires either shards from Legendary Recruitment, seasonal battle passes, or SSR→UR promotion.

Shard sources (per Theria Games, LDShop, Cpt Hedge):

  • Tavern — daily free recruits (mostly SR fodder, occasional SSR).
  • Elite Recruitment — SSR-guaranteed with SSR + UR shard mix.
  • Advanced (Legendary) Recruitment — UR-focused pool with ~1% UR rate, ~4% SSR rate.
  • Hero Wish — spend 200 wish tickets → 10 shards of a specific named hero. Primary way to farm a target UR without RNG.
  • Campaign farming — targeted SSR shards drop from specific stages. Mason (S1 promotion candidate) drops at Normal Stage 4-3 (1–2 per clear); Violet (S2) drops at Normal Stage 8-5 (1–3 per clear).
  • Trade & Plunder — attacking trucks yields ~2 shards each.
  • Honor Store, Campaign Store, Alliance Store — periodic hero shard rotations.
  • Battle Pass — every season includes a Growth / Hero pass with named UR shards on tracks.
  • Radar & event stores — Arms Race, Alliance Duels, Capital War all pay out shard bundles as milestones.
  • Black Market (post-season) — currency accumulated over the season is spent here for shard choice boxes; the primary F2P long-tail source.

The only universally-endorsed promotion is Mason (his Tank DPS is genuinely competitive in mono-Tank comps and shards drop from long-standing farm sources). Violet is conditional — worthwhile if she's already in your active squad, wasteful otherwise. Community consensus (Packsify, Handbook, Tap-Guides) is to skip Scarlett, Sarah, Venom, and Braz promotions unless you specifically need that seasonal comp.

Per Lootbar's Wall of Honor guide, published bonus types per hero:

  • Mason: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)
  • Cage: +0.50% Defense
  • Lucius: +0.50% Defense
  • Carlie: +0.50% Health
  • Tesla: +0.25% Attack
  • DVA: +0.50% Defense
  • Morrison: +0.50% Attack
  • Schuyler: +0.50% Attack
  • Violet: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)
  • Maxwell: +0.25% Attack
  • Fiona: +0.50% Attack
  • Swift: +0.50% Attack
  • Kimberly: +0.50% Attack [Last War Vault Wall of Honor Guide, 2026-07-15; corroborated by lastwar.wiki Wall of Honor page, 2026-07-15 — corrects a prior KB entry that read "+0.50% Defense", which was a KB transcription error; Kimberly is the flagship AoE-DPS Tank and grants the strongest tank Attack passive in the Wall of Honor]
  • Scarlett: +0.50% ATK + HP (UR-eligible)

2026-07-15 cross-check of Tank-hero bonuses — Last War Vault's Wall of Honor guide (Tier B) publishes the full Tank block as: Kimberly / Mason (UR) → +0.50% Attack; Marshall / Stetmann → +0.50% Defense; Murphy / Williams / Violet (UR) → +0.50% Health; Loki / Gump → +1.00% Troop Load. lastwar.wiki confirms Kimberly = Attack. This resolves the "Kimberly = Defense" question flagged in the KB's Contradictions section.

F2P balanced build (no UR-heavy investment):

  • Williams / Murphy / Kimberly / Marshall / Mason — "Murphy provides secondary frontline stability with HP and ATK buffs; Kimberly delivers AoE damage from behind the dual-tank wall" [packsify.com, 2026]. Mason is the F2P entry-point Tank DPS (SSR→UR-eligible via Season 1 promotion).

Medal refund reminder: SSR → UR promotion refunds 100% of pre-promotion medals. This is what makes early SSR-medal investment safe on promotion-eligible heroes (Mason, Violet, Scarlett, Sarah, Venom, Braz) — but ONLY if you actually plan to execute the promotion.

  • No level cap — every extra shard past 5★ compounds indefinitely.
  • Account-wide passive — bonuses apply whether the hero is fielded or benched.
  • Refund on promotion — SSR → UR promotion refunds shards spent on Wall entries for that hero, so investing Wall levels into Mason/Violet/etc. is zero-risk if you plan to promote them [lootbar.com wall-of-honor guide, 2026].
  • Every 50 Wall levels the per-tier bonus increases by +0.25% to +1.0% — meaning a hero at Wall Lv.100 has a materially stronger bonus than at Lv.50, and Lv.250 stronger still [lootbar.com, 2026; heaven-guardian.com wall-of-honor guide, 2026].
