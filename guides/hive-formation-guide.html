A Last War: Survival alliance is a 100-player cooperative unit built on a five-rank hierarchy (R5 → R1), a shared research tree paid in coin and diamond donations, a construction/research "help" mechanic that shaves seconds-to-hours off every teammate's timers, and a geographic clustering pattern ("the hive") that is the substrate for every alliance-level event and defense. The most rewarding alliance activity outside PvP is Marshal's Guard (Alliance Exercise), a 30-minute rally-marathon against five NPC tanks positioned inside a 3×3-tile event platform at the center of the hive (the LWS_Alliance_Ops_Center Hive Grid Manager plans this layout); the largest capturable seasonal objective is the 21×21-tile Military Stronghold, and Ring 1 members must sit against its outer mud to deny enemy landing zones. Alliance Duel (VS) is the recurring 6-day cross-server tournament (Mon–Sat, first to 7 victory points wins) that pairs with the Arms Race event and is the primary way alliances compete for server-level status. R5 assigns the four titled R4 slots — Warlord, Recruiter, Muse, Butler — and in Season 6 these titles gate the four alliance skills (Serpent Breath, Snake Barrier, Night Army, Thunder Feathers). The R4 cap starts at 8 and rises to 10 at Alliance Gift Level 10.

Positioning. The 3×3 event platform is placed as close to the alliance hive as possible to minimize march time. Rally leaders (R5, titled R4s, top-power R3s) sit in Ring 1 against the platform. See "Hive formations" below.

Hive formations

The "hive" is the geographic clustering of alliance bases around alliance buildings (turrets, flag) and event objects (Marshal's Guard platform, Military Stronghold). Hive discipline is one of the most-ignored and most-consequential alliance rules — a scattered alliance loses defensive reinforcement coverage, march-time-limited events like Marshal's Guard, and Zombie Siege reinforcement chains.

Base and event object sizes (tiles).

  • Player HQ / city: 2×2 tiles per base footprint (per hive-planner tools; note some sources treat member "cells" as 3×3 in planner UIs where one cell = one base + 1-tile buffer).
  • Marshal's Guard platform: 3×3 tiles (1 event cell).
  • Alliance Center (world building placement varies; the on-base structure is 5-tile-footprint per planners): 5×5 tiles.
  • Missile silo (turret/rocket world object): 4×4 tiles.
  • Military Stronghold seasonal fortress: 21×21 tiles (a 7-cell × 7-cell block in a 3-tile-per-cell planner grid).

Marshal's Guard hive shape.

  • Center = the 3×3 event platform (1 planner cell).
  • Recommended surrounding grid: 10×10 cells.
  • Members pack tightly against the platform and each other, no gaps needed for this event.
  • Ring capacity ≈ ring distance × 8 (a single ring around the 3×3 center has 8 slots; 2nd ring 16; 3rd ring 24).
  • Strongest players (R5 → titled R4 → untitled R4 → R3 by total power) fill Ring 1 first so their rallies enjoy shorter march time.

Military Stronghold hive shape (defensive).

The Stronghold is a 21×21-tile fortress surrounded by mud. Any enemy who lands within 9 tiles of the mud can rally the Stronghold — so the defensive job of the hive is to occupy those tiles so densely that no enemy can teleport a 3×3 city into a gap.

The convention used by the Alliance Hive Grid Manager tool (and echoed by most competitive alliances) is:

  • 2-tile empty gap between every pair of alliance members, in every direction, and between rings. This gap is narrow enough to deny a 3×3 enemy footprint but wide enough that a legitimate teammate can land there if needed.
  • Corner-anchored rings: each ring is a hollow rectangular frame whose four corners are always occupied. Inner edges pack at pitch 5 tiles (3-tile member + 2-tile gap) from corner to corner. The remaining edge stub may be 1 tile — still too narrow for an enemy 3×3 to slot in.

  • Rank ordering: R5 > titled R4 > untitled R4 > R3 by total power. Strongest inward. Ring 1 is where enemies land first if they crack through, so it needs the players with the fastest heal/repair and the highest defensive power.
  • Zero 3×3 empty zones anywhere. Any 3-tile square gap is a teleport landing pad.

Assembly Point (alliance flag). R5 sets the Alliance Assembly Point marker. Alliance Teleporter items snap you to the tile closest to that marker. All alliance members see the marker. First-time joiners auto-teleport to it. The flag is what makes teleport-into-hive one-click; move it near the current event object for whatever the alliance is coordinating that day.

Military Stronghold (요새) — capture rules

Season 1 seasonal building. 21×21-tile fortress guarded by NPC infected zombies scaled to the stronghold's level.

  • Capture cap: 2 Military Strongholds per alliance per day (global across all warzones).
  • Adjacency rule: you cannot capture an isolated stronghold — you must own an adjacent stronghold or (in some warzones) an outpost connected to a Level 1 or 2 Military Stronghold.
  • Requirement: Virus Resistance stat.
  • Alliance stronghold cap starts at 3. Each captured city adds capacity: +1 per L1–L5 city, +2 per L6 city.
  • Protection after capture: 36 hours.
  • Abandon: takes 1 hour. If protection expires mid-abandon, the abandon countdown continues.
  • Defending doesn't consume your daily capture cap.
