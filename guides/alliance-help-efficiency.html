Alliance Systems

Last researched: 2026-07-14 Sources consulted: 24 (see sources) KR sources included: ✓ Completeness: comprehensive

A Last War: Survival alliance is a 100-player cooperative unit built on a five-rank hierarchy (R5 → R1), a shared research tree paid in coin and diamond donations, a construction/research "help" mechanic that shaves seconds-to-hours off every teammate's timers, and a geographic clustering pattern ("the hive") that is the substrate for every alliance-level event and defense. The most rewarding alliance activity outside PvP is Marshal's Guard (Alliance Exercise), a 30-minute rally-marathon against five NPC tanks positioned inside a 3×3-tile event platform at the center of the hive (the LWS_Alliance_Ops_Center Hive Grid Manager plans this layout); the largest capturable seasonal objective is the 21×21-tile Military Stronghold, and Ring 1 members must sit against its outer mud to deny enemy landing zones. Alliance Duel (VS) is the recurring 6-day cross-server tournament (Mon–Sat, first to 7 victory points wins) that pairs with the Arms Race event and is the primary way alliances compete for server-level status. R5 assigns the four titled R4 slots — Warlord, Recruiter, Muse, Butler — and in Season 6 these titles gate the four alliance skills (Serpent Breath, Snake Barrier, Night Army, Thunder Feathers). The R4 cap starts at 8 and rises to 10 at Alliance Gift Level 10.

Last War alliances have five ranks. Only R5 and R4 carry mechanical permissions; R1–R3 are labels that alliances use internally to signal activity or trust.

R5 — Leader (연맹장). Exactly one per alliance. R5 has full authority: promote/demote anyone (including R4), kick anyone, accept/reject applicants, edit alliance settings (name, tag, join mode, description), disband, transfer leadership, assign the four alliance titles, place/destroy alliance buildings, declare wars, activate the R5-locked alliance skills, and set the Alliance Assembly Point (the anchor that alliance teleporters and free join-teleports snap to). During Marshal's Guard the R5's own rallies get a bonus damage buff (see the "R5 vs R4 titled vs R4 untitled" numbers below).

R4 — Officer (임원/운영진). Default cap of 8, rising to 10 once Alliance Gift Level reaches 10. R4s can accept applicants, kick R3-and-below, promote/demote R1–R3 (but not other R4s), build alliance turrets/flags, assign rally captain roles, send Alliance Mail (unlocked at Gift Level 5), and — if they hold one of the four titles — activate the title-locked alliance skill. R4s cannot kick or demote other R4s and cannot disband. Titled R4s carry a rally-damage buff untitled R4s do not (see numbers section).

The four R4 titles that R5 can assign are Warlord (전쟁군주), Recruiter (모집관), Muse (여신), and Butler (집사). In lore they map to attack/defense, member intake, morale, and daily housekeeping respectively — but in-game they are load-bearing because each is glued to exactly one alliance skill in Season 6 and each provides the same +5% rally-damage buff during Marshal's Guard. Assigning a title costs nothing and takes seconds; the reliable pattern is "assign a title to every R4 before every Alliance Exercise."

R3, R2, R1 — Members (일반 유저). No mechanical differences between these three ranks. Alliances use them as visual labels: R3 usually means "trusted rally leader," R2 means "active contributor," R1 means "new/probationary/inactive." All three can request help, receive help, tap help for others, teleport to the alliance flag, donate to alliance techs, join rallies, participate in Marshal's Guard, and reinforce members' bases from the Alliance Center. Whether an R3 can lead a rally that is expected to hit hard is a social decision, not a permission.

Alliance size and membership

Hard cap: 100 members. Once full, R5 or an R4 must kick someone before a new applicant can be accepted. There is no way to grow past 100. Serious alliances rotate inactive slots aggressively — alliance score in Kill Events, VS Days and Marshal's Guard is the sum of every occupied slot's output, so a dead R1 costs the whole alliance.

Joining is either open (auto-accept), application, or invite-only. First-time joiners get a free teleport to the Alliance Assembly Point. There is also a free Enemy Buster-day teleport every Saturday to the duel warzone.

Minimum alliance size to participate in Alliance Duel VS is 20 members (per LDPlayer's Korean guide).
