Alliance Flag Generator. Free. In your browser.

Type your alliance tag, describe the emblem in plain English ("gold crown on dark shield", "silver wolf blue moon", "rust axe on tan"), pick a season theme, and download a 400×400 PNG. Use it as a Discord server icon, a channel header, a tool background, or a message-thread avatar for your alliance chat. Everything runs client-side — no upload, no account, no watermark on the flag itself beyond a tiny r5tools.io credit in the corner.

Why this feature exists

The Roster Extractor pipeline is starting to produce clean per-alliance lists — full members, ranks, power tiers. The natural next step: give each of those alliances a shareable brand asset they can slap onto their Discord, their internal dashboards, their VS-day recap posts. Most alliances end up using a random game screenshot or a Google Image find for their icon; this is a dedicated way to spin one up in under a minute.

How the emblem gets picked:

  • Keyword parse. The description is scanned for known nouns — crown, shield, sword, star, bolt, flame, moon, sun, wolf, dragon, arrow, leaf, skull, eye, snake, phoenix, anchor, heart, axe, hammer, horse, tower. First match wins.
  • Color parse. Words like gold, red, blue, emerald, violet, silver, onyx, rust, copper get pulled in order — the first tints the emblem; on the custom theme the second and third tint the badge fill + ring.
  • Composition. Circular badge, ring border, emblem glyph centered, alliance tag in a rounded banner underneath. Same scaffolding every time — just the colors + glyph swap.
