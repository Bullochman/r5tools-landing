Season 2 (marketed as Polar Storm, sometimes Arctic Storm or Frozen) is Last War: Survival's second seasonal ruleset, replacing Season 1's virus-stack mechanic with an active per-tile temperature system. The map is a frozen concentric grid running from -15 °C at the outer corners to -80 °C at the Capitol, with per-band drops of roughly 15 °C per zone; if a base's temperature drops below -20 °C for 5 minutes it enters the Frozen state, which disables rally, teleport, and relocation (see 04-squads-combat for rally mechanics and 08-alliance-systems for hive coordination implications). Survival is driven by three new buildings — the personal High-Heat Furnace (level 30 max, coal-burning, adds +5.5 °C to +20 °C activation / +8 °C to +30 °C overdrive), the shared Alliance Furnace (level 20 max, R4/R5 deployed on Day 1, produces +3 °C to +12.5 °C normal / up to +25 °C overdrive across a shared radius), and the Titanium Alloy Plant (level 30 max, +720 titanium/hr per level, up to 4 factories organically + 1 from weekly pass). Territory is captured for Rare Soil (the seasonal ranking currency) — max 6 cities + 4 dig sites per alliance, 2 captures per day, 36-hour post-capture protection, city temperature bonus scaling +5 °C at L1 → +60 °C at L6 — culminating in the Rare Soil War in Week 4 where factions can plunder 20–30 % of enemy soil stocks under a -70 °C blizzard.

If exactly one of the above six items is unresolved by end of Day 1, prioritize Alliance Furnace deployment — every other seasonal decision is downstream of the furnace being live.

  1. Fire your own High-Heat Furnace (or flip it to overdrive) so your local heat output overrides the ambient chill.
  2. Ally spy plane — an alliance-mate can fly a spy plane over your base with heating enabled to raise your local temperature and initiate defrost. This is the primary rescue method when a member's own coal has run out. [lastwartutorial.com search snippet, lastwar-tutorial.com]
  3. Move within range of an active Alliance Furnace or captured city — passive but slow because relocation itself is disabled while Frozen. In practice this only works if the base was pre-positioned before it froze.
  • Purpose: projects heat across a shared radius covering every alliance base parked inside its range. This is what makes hive discipline mandatory in Season 2.
  • Deployment: an R4 or R5 officer places the Alliance Furnace spot on a Level 1 territory on Day 1 of the season. It requires up to 20 reinforcements to activate (an alliance mini-construction event). There is no build-time delay after activation — it is active as soon as it is deployed. [lastwartutorial.com, lastwarguide.net]
  • Max level: 20.
  • Upgrade path: automatic. As the alliance accumulates Rare Soil in its stockpile (via captured cities and dig sites), the Alliance Furnace's level rises automatically once thresholds are crossed. Officers do not click an upgrade button. [lastwartutorial.com]
  • Heat output:
  • L1: +3 °C normal / +6 °C overdrive (approx; overdrive doubles normal)
  • L20: +12.5 °C normal / +25 °C overdrive
  • Coal consumption at L20: 1,440 coal/min normal, 5,760 coal/min overdrive (the ~4× overdrive ratio). Members donate coal (up to 50 donations/day per member) to keep it burning. [lastwartutorial.com, medievalfun.com]
  • Range: the furnace produces heat across a wide square area ("squared tile"); every base whose tile falls within that area receives the full heat bonus regardless of position within the zone. The exact tile radius by level is not published on any surveyed source — descriptions are qualitative ("wide radius", "increases with level", "boosted by alliance season research"). This is a known gap; see Open Questions. [medievalfun.com, ldshop.gg]
  • Relocation: the Alliance Furnace can be moved later at significant resource cost. Alliance leaders should treat it as a movable strategic asset — recommended relocation cadence is to push it deeper toward the Capitol as the alliance's furnace level and heat coverage grow. [lastwarguide.net]

The Alliance Furnace is also the war objective for the Rare Soil War (see Week 4 below). Its durability is the win condition; when attackers reduce it to 0 durability during a war window, they win. Up to 100 squads can reinforce it during a defense.

  1. Reposition the hive if the drop location is too crowded or too close to hostile alliances. The whole hive can be moved using the free alliance-relocation trick (leaders shift the Assembly Point; members teleport back and forth without spending personal teleports).   3. Garrison the Alliance Furnace with as many players as possible to burn through the ~20 reinforcements gate quickly.
  4. Every member builds their own High-Heat Furnace and Titanium Alloy Factory #1 and enables the auto-blizzard toggle.
  5. Weaker players park closer to the Alliance Furnace; stronger players anchor the outer ring so they can absorb hits and provide reinforcement.
  6. Do not kill the highest-level Wanderer / Doom Walker before the map transition — the coal reward only pays out post-transition. Community reports a ~200,000 coal payout for a level 180 Wanderer first-kill after transition. [dcinside gall/12212]
  7. Stack radar missions before the season opens so they can be cleared on Day 1 for guaranteed coal rewards. [lastwartutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide]

Unlocked in Week 4, the Rare Soil War is a scheduled cross-alliance combat event whose objective is to destroy the enemy Alliance Furnace and plunder up to 20 % of their Rare Soil stockpile (30 % after the Showdown event later in the season). [lastwarguide.net, lastwartutorial.com]

Warlord Missile: high-level attackers can launch a Warlord Missile at an enemy Alliance Furnace:

  • 3-minute (180-second) preparation window after launch during which the Warlord's own base is revealed to the entire server via world-chat broadcast and the Warlord enters "War Fever" status. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15; lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]
  • Launch can be attempted during both the Preparation Stage and the Combat Stage of a war round, not only during the 30-minute Prep. [lastwar-tutorial.com Ultimate Strategy Guide, 2026-07-15]
  • Impact zone: 25×25 tile grid ("Contaminated Land") in Season 2 that lasts 1 hour, during which bases inside cannot activate shields. Furnace takes 30,000 durability damage on a successful impact. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15]
  • Season 3 blast size expansion (added 2026-07-16): Season 3 Golden Kingdom / Spice Wars / Faction Duel expands the same Warlord Missile mechanic to a 35×35 tile blast. Season 2 remains 25×25. Any downstream Warlord Missile simulator tool that spans multiple seasons must key blast size off the current season. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 3 Spice Wars & Faction Duel Complete Guide search-excerpt 2026-07-16 Tier B; direct fetch 403]
  • Multiple Warlords from allied attacker alliances can queue simultaneous missiles at the same furnace. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15]

Close-perimeter defender ping — the mechanic the KB was missing. On successful impact, every base inside the 25×25 blast area is forcibly relocated to a random position on the map — except the Warlord's own base if it happens to be inside the radius. This is the "close-perimeter ping" observed in community reports: defenders reinforcing the Alliance Furnace do not merely lose their shield — the game physically evicts them from their reinforcement position, forcing the furnace to be re-reinforced under fire while defenders find their new (often deep-frozen) landing tiles, shield up, and teleport back. Reinforcement squads inside the furnace itself are not evicted (they are inside the building, not on a tile), but any base staged next to the furnace to launch counter-rallies is displaced. Shields are also blocked inside the Contaminated Land for the full 1-hour duration, so re-shielding at the random landing tile is possible but re-shielding at any tile inside the 25×25 zone is not. [lastwar-tutorial.com Rare Earth War, 2026-07-15; lastwarguide.net Season 2, 2026-07-15]

Defensive positioning consequence. Because the 25×25 ping evicts everyone in the blast area, the standard defense guidance is: park every base at least 5 tiles from the Alliance Furnace tile (outside the 25×25 blast when centered on the furnace, which corresponds to roughly a ring 5-12 tiles out depending on furnace footprint). The Assembly Point should be placed near the furnace but outside the missile blast radius so evicted defenders have a reliable return tile to shield up and teleport back from. [lastwar-guide.org Season 2 Week 4, 2026-07-15]

  1. Week-1 speed matters more than late-season power spikes. An alliance that deploys its Alliance Furnace and captures its first L1 Dig Site within Hour 12 of Day 1 will out-accumulate an alliance that spends Week 1 optimizing before its first capture — even if the second alliance ends up with more combat power in Week 6.
  2. Rare Soil War plunder is worth less than steady-state production. A successful 20% plunder of an enemy's stockpile shifts soil between alliances but does not create new soil; over 8 weeks, the alliance that spends less time attacking and more time farming captured territory typically wins.
  3. Do not over-capture into a higher bracket you cannot defend. Rare Soil War brackets (Ranks 1-10, 11-20, 21-30, 31-50) are set by stockpile — capturing extra cities can promote you into a tougher bracket where you get plundered more than you can produce. [lastwarhandbook.com Season 2 Guide, 2026-07-15]
