I've been talking to R5s from a few top-100 alliances about how they actually use r5tools day-to-day.

This one stuck. I'm going to anonymize the alliance name (they asked) and call them "TK" for this email. TK is currently top-50 on their warzone, competes for FC (Frost Cathedral) every rotation, and their R5 credits the [Heat Simulator](https://r5tools.io/heat-simulator) with saving them from at least three bad-timing pushes this season.

---

**The problem TK was solving:**

FC pushes are heat-gated. Alliance heat spikes when you rally, spikes again when you take a target, decays over hours. Push while your heat is above the threshold and you eat a damage penalty that can flip a winnable fight. Their old approach was "Discord ping, everyone log in, R5 says go" — which meant sometimes they pushed at 87% heat and sometimes at 32%, and nobody could reliably predict the difference.

**What they do now:**

Every Sunday, TK's R5 pulls up Heat Simulator, punches in:
- Their alliance's current heat baseline (visible in-game — TK reads it off two officers' screens and averages)
- Planned rallies for the week (5 major ones, ~7 sub-rallies)
- FC rotation schedule (posted by LWS)

The sim outputs a heat-over-time chart with the "safe push" green bands overlaid. TK's R5 screenshots it, pins it in Discord, and every officer knows: "we push in the green band Thursday 21:30 UTC, not before."

**Result:** they've hit 4 of the last 4 FC rotations. Prior to using the sim they were hitting ~2 of 4. Doubling FC captures matters — that's the difference between top-100 and top-50 on their warzone.
