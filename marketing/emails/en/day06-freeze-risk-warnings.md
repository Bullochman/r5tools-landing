The [Freeze Risk Dashboard](https://r5tools.io/freeze-risk) surfaces about a dozen different warnings. Three of them are the ones alliances actually get bricked by. I'm going to walk through each one with a real (anonymized) example.

---

**Warning #1: "Underpowered edge cell"**

*What it means:* A member is sitting on a hex adjacent to a rival alliance's front line, and their power is more than 20% below the median alliance power. They are a soft target and will get hit first when the freeze window opens.

*Real example:* One of TK's officers (top-50 alliance from Day 3's email) got flagged on the dashboard 4 hours before a freeze window. The alliance shielded her manually. When the freeze hit, she took 3 attempted rallies from a rival KR alliance — all bounced off the shield. Without the alert, she would've been bricked. Cost of the shield: negligible. Cost of getting bricked: 8+ hours of rebuild time in the middle of a critical season week.

---

**Warning #2: "Depot inside enemy rally range"**

*What it means:* An alliance depot is placed within rally-march distance of a rival alliance's known aggressive R4s. This is a slow-burn warning — the depot isn't in danger *right now*, but as soon as freeze hits, it will be.

*Real example:* An alliance I'll call "MV" put their secondary depot in what looked like a safe spot on the S2 map. The Freeze Risk Dashboard flagged it as "inside rally range of 3 hostile R4s within 4 hours." MV's R5 ignored it. Freeze window opened. Depot was rallied 3 times in 6 hours, dropped, and MV lost their reinforcement chain for the rest of the day. When they moved the depot post-mortem, the dashboard stopped warning.

*The lesson isn't "the dashboard is smart." The lesson is: **when it warns, act.***

---

**Warning #3: "Member is offline + full resources"**

*What it means:* A member hasn't logged in for 24+ hours *and* their resource stockpile is above the alliance median. They're a fat, immobile target that will get farmed the moment the freeze window opens.

*Real example:* This is the most common one. A whale who bought a big resource pack, then went on a business trip. Dashboard flagged it. R5 messaged the whale, whale spent 30 seconds on shields, whale returned from trip to find his base untouched instead of bricked and drained. Whale renewed for the next season.
