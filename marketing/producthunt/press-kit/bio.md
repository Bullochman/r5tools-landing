## Long (~600 chars — for feature articles)

Evan Jones is the R5 (top-rank alliance leader) of RONY / TINO alliance in Last War: Survival's Warzone 2007, a Korean-language dominant server. After losing a Season 2 campaign because his 100-member alliance was coordinating landing coordinates through an auto-translated Google Sheet, he spent three months building r5tools.io — a suite of 11 alliance planning tools ranging from a Claude-vision-powered Roster Extractor (turns a 90-second phone video of the in-game member list into a 100-player CSV in 2 minutes) to a live Freeze Risk dashboard used during the game's Polar Storm season. r5tools.io ships in 7 languages, is free for Warzone 2007 alliances, and now serves R5s in dozens of warzones. Evan is a full-time software developer by day; r5tools.io is a nights-and-weekends project born out of the specific pain of playing an East Asian mobile game across a language barrier.

## Product description — for "About the product" boilerplate

r5tools.io is a suite of 11 alliance planning tools for the mobile strategy game Last War: Survival. It is designed for R5s (top-rank alliance leaders) and their officers who need to coordinate 100 players across 8-week seasons, and is the only toolkit in the category built Korean-first (the language spoken in the majority of the game's most competitive warzones). Tools include a Claude-vision Roster Extractor, a Season 2 Landing Planner, a live Freeze Risk dashboard, a Coal Burn calculator, a Hive Grid formation planner, a City Capture scheduler, a Season Timeline, and more. The suite is free for the maker's own warzone and available at $10 (personal) / $50 (alliance) for others.
